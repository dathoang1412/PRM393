Request 1 : Project Charter Statement
1. Title of project:
Coi Cafe Central Vietnam Flagship Expansion Project 
2. Rationale:
Coi Cafe has so far opened 12 stores in Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City.
The project will assist Coi Cafe in enhancing its brand presence in Central Vietnam.
The five target provinces are Da Nang, Hue, Quang Nam, Quang Ngai and Binh Dinh.
The central region of Vietnam has high potential thanks to its tourism, urbanization and increasing demand for coffee shops.
The project promotes the sustainable business model of Coi Cafe thanks to the application of reusable cups and direct coffee purchase from farmers in Dak Lak.
The expansion can help boost revenue and strengthen Coi Cafe as a Vietnamese coffee brand nationally.
3. Project limitations:
Scope restriction:
5 flagship stores to be opened, one in each target province.
Full premises selection, interior design, construction, recruitment, training, supply chain setup and marketing campaigns.
Time limit:
All five stores are to be opened within 12 months.
Cost constraint: 
Total budget is limited to 8.5 billion VND.
Average budget is around 1.7 billion VND per shop.
Quality requirement:
The stores should reflect the nostalgic and sustainable brand concept of Coi Cafe.
All stores are required to offer the same level of service and quality of drinks.
Each store must achieve break-even point in 8 months after opening.

Request 2: Major Cost/Budget Items
1. Inspection of premises and deposit for rent
Study of the market, verification of customer traffic, legal check, deposits for rental of five stores.
Estimated cost: 1.5 billion VND.
How it is estimated: Based on expected rental deposits and market rental prices in each province.
Arrested: Director of Market Development.
2. Design and Construction of the Interior
Description: Store layout, decoration, furniture, lighting, counter area, nostalgic Vietnamese coffee design.
Cost estimate: 3.0 billion dong.
Estimate method : Estimated about600 million/store (contractor quotation basis)
Responsible Person: Interior Design Manager.
3. Equipment and materials 
Description: Coffee machines, grinders, refrigerators, POS systems, reusable cups, tables, chairs, kitchen tools.
Estimated cost: 1,300,000,000 VND.
Estimation Method : Supplier price quotations and historical store purchase data.
Charged person: Operations Manager
4. Hiring and training
Description: Recruiting baristas, store managers, service staff, and arranging training sessions.
Estimated cost: 700 million VND.
Estimate Method: Expected employees per store and training cost per store.
Person charged: HR Manager. 
5. Marketing campaign launches
Social media ads, grand opening events, local KOL/KOC promotion, posters, vouchers, customer activities.
Estimated cost: 1.0 billion VND.
Estimate way: Around 200 million VND/store
Marketing Manager charged.
6. Contingency fund
Description: Contingency for unexpected increase in costs, construction delay or change of supplier.
Estimated cost: 1,000,000,000 VND.
Estimate Method: Approx 12% of total budget
Charged Person: Project Manager
Total estimated budget: 8.5 billion VND

Request 3.Project risks 
1. Risk of construction delays 
Description: 
Delay in interior construction may be due to contractor problems or material shortages.
Possible effects:
Time: Store opening could be late 
Cost: There may be extra costs for labor or rental
Quality: Construction may be rushed and lower store quality.
Mitigation plan: 
Employ skilled contractors.
prepare a detailed building schedule.
Monitor construction progress on a weekly basis.
Plan B: 
Employ backup contractors.
Open stores in phases if some stosers are late.
2: risk: inappropriate location selection
Description: 
Some stores may be in areas with low customer traffic or weak demand.
Potential impact: 
Scope/quality: the store may not achieve the intnded market coverage.
TIme: the beak-even period maybe longer.
Cost higher marketing and rental costs.
Mitigation plan:
Boost local promotion.
Review lease and renegotiate lease term or move.
3. Risk: Varied quality of service
Description
New local staff may not deliver the same standard of service as exisiting store staff.
potential effect: 
Quality: customer satisfaction may decline
Cost" Addtional training and supervision maybe neccesart
Time: perfomance may gradually improve.
response plan:
Standardized training.
Use check lists and serrvices manuals.
Backup plan:
Suport new stores a the lauch time by sending experienced staff from Hanoi or HO CHI MINH city.
