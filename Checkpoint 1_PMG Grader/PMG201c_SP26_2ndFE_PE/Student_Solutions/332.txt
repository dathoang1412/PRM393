Request 1 Narrative Charter Statement
- Project name: Coi Cafe Central Vietnam Flagship Expansion Project.
- Justifications: Coi Cafe is a Vietnamese coffee brand with 12 stores in Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City. The brand is known for nostalgic store design, traditional Vietnamese drinks, reusable cups, and direct sourcing from Dak Lak farmers. This project aims to open five flagship stores in Da Nang, Hue, Quang Nam, Quang Ngai, and Binh Dinh within 12 months.
- The project is justified because the Central region has strong tourism, developing urban areas, and increasing demand for local coffee experiences. The expansion will help Coi Cafe grow market share, build brand awareness, increase revenue, and create a model for future provincial expansion. It also supports the company’s sustainable image through reusable cups and direct farmer partnerships.
- Project constraints: The scope is limited to five flagship stores only. The scope includes site selection, store design, construction, staff recruitment, staff training, supply chain setup, and local launch campaigns. Opening more than five stores or changing the core business model is outside the scope.
- The time limit is 12 months for opening all stores. Each store must reach break-even within 8 months after opening. The total cost limit is VND 8.5 billion, funded by company equity and bank loans. The quality requirement is that all stores must maintain Coi Cafe’s nostalgic atmosphere, traditional menu, reusable cup system, and sustainable sourcing model.

Request 2 Main Cost and Budget Items
Site survey and lease preparation: This includes market research, location visits, traffic checking, competitor analysis, legal review, and deposits. Estimated cost is VND 550 million. The method is market comparison. Person in charge is the Project Manager.
Interior design and construction: This includes layout design, construction, decoration, lighting, counter setup, seating area, and signage. Estimated cost is VND 3.85 billion. The method is analogous estimation from previous stores. Person in charge is the Interior Design Lead.
Equipment and furniture: This includes coffee machines, grinders, refrigerators, tables, chairs, POS system, reusable cups, and cup washing stations. Estimated cost is VND 1.25 billion. The method is bottom-up estimation from supplier quotations. Person in charge is the Procurement Team.
Recruitment and training: This includes hiring store managers, baristas, and service staff, then training them on recipes, customer service, and sustainability procedures. Estimated cost is VND 700 million. The method is HR cost estimation based on staff number and training duration. Person in charge is the HR Team.
Supply chain and initial inventory: This includes supplier contracts, logistics setup, coffee beans, tea, milk, packaging, and first inventory for each store. Estimated cost is VND 650 million. The method is bottom-up estimation. Person in charge is the Procurement and Finance Team.
Launch marketing: This includes social media ads, opening events, vouchers, local influencers, and promotion materials. Estimated cost is VND 500 million. The method is percentage-of-budget estimation. Person in charge is the Marketing Team.
Project administration: This includes travel, meetings, reports, legal permits, and project coordination. Estimated cost is VND 300 million. The method is analogous estimation. Person in charge is the Project Manager.
Contingency reserve: This is used for unexpected price increases, delays, or emergency support. Estimated cost is VND 700 million. The method is risk-based percentage estimation. Person in charge is the Project Manager and Finance Team.

Total estimated budget: VND 8.5 billion.

2)
Request 3 Three Project Risks

- Risk 1 Construction delay: Construction may be delayed due to contractor problems, local permit issues, material delivery, or bad weather. This can affect scope and quality because stores may be rushed or simplified. It can delay opening by 1 to 3 months and increase cost by VND 300 million to VND 500 million. Mitigation is to pre-select backup contractors and monitor progress weekly. Contingency is to use prefabricated furniture and switch contractors when needed.
- Risk 2 Poor staff readiness: Local staff may not meet Coi Cafe’s drink and service standards. This can reduce quality, delay opening by 2 to 4 weeks, and add VND 150 million to VND 250 million for retraining. Mitigation is to send experienced trainers from existing stores. Contingency is to assign floating supervisors to support weak stores.
- Risk 3 Failure to break even: Some stores may not reach break-even within 8 months because of weak customer traffic or strong competitors. This affects project objectives, may require more time for adjustment, and may add VND 200 million to VND 400 million in marketing support. Mitigation is to conduct detailed site research and monitor sales daily. Contingency is to increase promotions, adjust operating hours, and negotiate rent support.
