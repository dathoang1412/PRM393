Request 1:

Project name:
Coi Cafe Central Youth Market Expansion Project

Justifications:
Expand Coi Cafe into five Central provinces: Da Nang, Hue, Quang Nam, Quang Ngai, and Binh Dinh.
Target young customer groups such as students, young office workers, and first-jobbers in growing urban areas.
Use flagship stores as attractive study, meeting, and social spaces while keeping Coi Cafe’s nostalgic Vietnamese coffee identity.
Promote traditional Vietnamese drinks, reusable cups, and direct coffee sourcing from Dak Lak farmers to younger customers.
Build long-term customer loyalty by entering the market early and creating regular customer habits.
Develop a stable Central region supply chain to support store operations.
Create new revenue outside Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City and help each store reach break-even within eight months after opening.

Project constraints:
Scope:
Open five flagship stores only, one store in each selected province.
Project scope includes: youth market survey, location selection, lease agreement, interior design, construction, equipment setup, local staff recruitment, training, supplier setup, student-focused launch campaign, and performance tracking.
The project does not include franchising, opening extra stores, online-only business, or new product line development.
Time:
The project duration is 12 months.
All five stores must be opened within the approved timeline.
Each store must reach break-even within eight months after opening.
Cost / Budget:
Total budget is VND 8.5 billion.
Average budget is approximately VND 1.7 billion per store.
Funding comes from company equity and commercial bank loans.
Cost must be controlled across rent, construction, equipment, human resources, supply chain, and marketing.
Quality:
All stores must follow Coi Cafe’s brand standards.
Store design must remain nostalgic but comfortable for studying, working, and meeting friends.
Drink quality, service, hygiene, and reusable cup practice must be consistent across all stores.
Marketing activities must fit Coi Cafe’s image and not make the brand look low-quality.

Request 3:

Risk 1: Youth customers are price-sensitive
Risk name: Price resistance from student and young customer groups.
Description: Young customers may like the store concept but may consider prices too high compared with local coffee shops or milk tea stores.
Impacts:
Scope / quality: The store may fail to become a regular destination for the target customer group.
Time: More time may be needed to build repeat visits.
Cost: More promotions or discounts may reduce profit margin.
Mitigation plan: Conduct price research before opening and design suitable product bundles without lowering core product quality.
Contingency plan: Offer student combos, loyalty cards, off-peak discounts, and affordable best-seller sets.

Risk 2: Store space is used heavily but sales are low
Risk name: Long stay time with low spending.
Description: Students and young customers may stay for long hours to study or meet friends but purchase only one low-value item.
Impacts:
Scope / quality: Seat availability and customer experience may be affected during peak hours.
Time: Store traffic may look high but sales growth may be slow.
Cost: Electricity, cleaning, and service costs may increase without enough revenue.
Mitigation plan: Design seating zones, encourage group orders, and create combo offers for longer-stay customers.
Contingency plan: Apply time-based promotions, minimum order guidance during peak hours, and take-away offers to improve sales.

Risk 3: Marketing trend changes quickly
Risk name: Youth-focused campaign loses attention quickly.
Description: Social media trends and young customer preferences may change fast, making launch content less effective.
Impacts:
Scope / quality: Brand communication may not attract enough attention.
Time: More time may be needed to rebuild awareness.
Cost: Additional content production and advertising costs may be required.
Mitigation plan: Monitor social media response weekly and use flexible content plans for each province.
Contingency plan: Refresh campaign messages, cooperate with local student communities, and use short-term viral challenges or events.
