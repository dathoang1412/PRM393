REQUEST 1

Project charter statement

Project name

Coi Cafe Central Vietnam Flagship Store Development Project

This project is planned to help Coi Cafe develop its business in the Central region of Vietnam. The main goal is to open five flagship stores in Da Nang, Hue, Quang Nam, Quang Ngai, and Binh Dinh within 12 months. Each store will be built as a full brand store, not only a small selling point. The stores must show the main image of Coi Cafe, including Vietnamese coffee, warm design, friendly service, and sustainable business practices.

The project will start with market study and location planning. After that, the company will choose suitable store spaces, negotiate rental contracts, design the stores, build the interiors, buy equipment, prepare raw materials, recruit local employees, train staff, and launch opening campaigns. The project will also include a trial operation period before the official opening so the team can check service quality, drink quality, equipment, and customer response.

The Market Development Director will act as the project manager. This person will manage the overall plan and make sure that each team completes its tasks on time. The project will involve the finance team, design and construction team, human resources team, marketing team, operations team, suppliers, contractors, and local store managers. The project manager will also be responsible for reporting progress to senior management and solving important problems during implementation.

Project justification

The project is important because Coi Cafe needs to expand its market and become stronger outside Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City. The company already has experience operating coffee stores in large cities. Therefore, moving into Central Vietnam is a suitable development step. This region has many potential customers, such as students, office workers, tourists, young people, and families.

Central Vietnam is also attractive because many cities in this area are developing quickly. Da Nang is a major tourism and business city. Hue has a strong cultural image and many students. Quang Nam, Quang Ngai, and Binh Dinh are also growing markets where modern coffee shops have more opportunities to develop. If Coi Cafe enters these areas with well designed flagship stores, the company can build customer trust and brand awareness early.

The project also supports the companys financial goals. New stores can create new revenue streams and reduce dependence on existing markets. If the stores perform well, Coi Cafe can continue expanding to other provinces using the experience from this project. The project can also help the company understand Central customers better and improve its future expansion strategy.

In addition, the project matches Coi Cafes brand values. The company promotes Vietnamese coffee culture and buys coffee from Dak Lak farmers. Opening more stores can increase demand for these coffee beans and support the local coffee supply chain. The use of reusable cups and sustainable practices can also make the brand more attractive to customers who care about the environment.

Project constraints

The first constraint is scope. The project is limited to five flagship stores only. These stores must be opened in Da Nang, Hue, Quang Nam, Quang Ngai, and Binh Dinh. The project does not include franchising, opening stores in other regions, or changing the companys main brand direction.

The second constraint is schedule. The project must be completed within 12 months. Because the project has many activities, the schedule must be planned carefully. Location selection, rental contracts, design, construction, recruitment, training, supply preparation, and marketing must be connected logically. A delay in one stage can cause delay in other stages.

The third constraint is budget. The total budget is VND 8.5 billion. This amount must be divided among five stores and all project activities. The team must avoid unnecessary spending and control all expenses through budget tracking. If costs increase too much, the company may need extra funding or may have to reduce some non essential items.

The fourth constraint is quality. Coi Cafe must keep its brand quality in every new store. Customers should receive the same drink quality, service style, and store atmosphere as in existing stores. The company should not open stores quickly if the stores are not ready, because poor quality can create negative customer reviews and hurt the brand image.


REQUEST 2

Main cost and budget items

1. Feasibility study cost

This cost is used to check whether each selected province is suitable for opening a flagship store. It includes market visits, customer observation, competitor analysis, rental price checking, and sales potential review. The estimate can be made by calculating travel costs, staff working days, survey tools, and possible local consultant fees. The Project Manager and Marketing team will handle this cost because they need to understand the market before investment decisions are made.

2. Rental contract cost

This cost includes rental deposits, advance rent, contract checking fees, and service charges from property agents if used. For a flagship coffee store, location is one of the most important success factors. The estimate can be made by comparing rental prices in different streets and commercial areas. The Finance team should work with the Project Manager to check whether the expected revenue can cover the rental cost.

3. Store construction cost

This cost includes renovation, electrical work, water system, floor, ceiling, wall painting, lighting, counter area, kitchen area, restroom, and signboard. Since each store must follow the same brand image, construction quality must be controlled carefully. The estimate can be made by asking contractors for detailed quotations and comparing prices by store size. The Design and Construction team will manage this cost and report to the Project Manager.

4. Decoration and brand display cost

This cost includes decoration items, wall pictures, menu boards, indoor plants, traditional style objects, brand signs, and other items that create the Coi Cafe atmosphere. This item is separated from construction because decoration strongly affects customer feeling. The estimate can be made by preparing a design list for each store and checking supplier prices. The Design team and Marketing team will support this estimate.

5. Equipment and technology cost

This cost includes espresso machines, coffee grinders, refrigerators, freezers, blenders, water filters, POS systems, internet setup, cameras, speakers, and payment devices. These items are necessary for daily store operation. The estimate can be made by creating a standard equipment package for one store and multiplying it for five stores. The Operations team and Procurement team will collect quotations and compare warranty conditions.

6. Employee preparation cost

This cost includes recruitment advertisement, interview process, uniforms, training salary, trainer cost, training documents, and practice materials. The new stores need employees who can make drinks correctly and serve customers politely. The HR team can estimate this cost by calculating the number of staff needed for each store and the length of the training period. The Operations team should also help define the staff structure for each store.

7. Opening promotion cost

This cost includes local advertising, online ads, grand opening activities, discount programs, gift vouchers, loyalty cards, posters, banners, and social media content. The goal of this cost is to make people know the new stores quickly. The estimate can be prepared by making a marketing plan for each province. The Marketing team will decide the budget based on customer groups, competition level, and store location.

8. First month operating supply cost

This cost includes coffee beans, milk, tea, sugar, syrup, cups, napkins, packaging, cleaning tools, and other materials needed for store operation. The estimate can be made by predicting the number of drinks sold in the first month and calculating the material needed for each drink. The Operations team will be responsible for this item and must make sure that every store has enough stock before opening.

9. Transportation and distribution cost

This cost includes moving equipment to stores, delivering ingredients, transferring supplies between provinces, and setting up regular delivery routes. Since the project covers five different locations, logistics must be planned carefully. The estimate can be made based on delivery distance, delivery frequency, supplier location, and storage needs. The Operations team and Procurement team will manage this cost.

10. Contingency and project monitoring cost

This cost includes unexpected expenses and the cost of monitoring the project. It may cover urgent repairs, small design changes, material price increases, extra delivery, or additional quality checks. The estimate can be around 5 percent to 10 percent of the total project budget. The Project Manager and Finance team will control this amount and only use it when there is a real need.


REQUEST 3

Project risks and response plans

Risk 1

The stores may not match local customer taste

Coi Cafe has a clear brand style, but customers in Central Vietnam may have different preferences from customers in Hanoi or Ho Chi Minh City. Some customers may prefer local coffee styles, lower prices, or different store atmospheres. If the company does not understand these differences, the stores may not attract enough repeat customers.

This risk can affect revenue because customers may visit once but not return. It can also affect cost because the company may need to spend more on promotions or menu adjustments. It can affect quality if the company changes products too quickly without proper testing.

The mitigation plan is to study customer taste in each province before opening. The Marketing team and Operations team should collect information about popular drinks, price levels, customer habits, and competitor menus. The contingency plan is to test a few local inspired drinks, adjust promotion packages, and collect customer feedback during the soft opening period.

Risk 2

Rental locations may be too expensive

A good store location can help attract customers, but it may also have very high rent. If Coi Cafe chooses expensive locations, the stores may have high fixed costs every month. This can make it difficult to reach break even, especially in the first months when customer awareness is still low.

This risk affects the budget and long term profitability. Even if the store has good sales, high rental cost can reduce profit. It may also create pressure on the company to increase prices, which may not be suitable for some local customers.

The mitigation plan is to compare several possible locations before signing contracts. The team should calculate expected sales, expected rent ratio, deposit, and contract conditions. The contingency plan is to choose a nearby second choice location with lower rent, negotiate a rent free setup period, or negotiate a better payment schedule with the landlord.

Risk 3

The project team may not coordinate well

This project involves many teams, including finance, design, HR, marketing, and operations. If communication is weak, one team may finish late or miss important information. For example, the marketing team may plan an opening event before the construction team finishes the store. The HR team may recruit staff before the training schedule is ready.

This risk affects time because tasks may need to be repeated or delayed. It affects cost because poor coordination can cause waste of money and resources. It affects quality because store opening may be rushed.

The mitigation plan is to create a clear project schedule and hold weekly project meetings. The Project Manager should update task status, deadlines, risks, and decisions regularly. The contingency plan is to create a fast decision process for urgent problems and assign one contact person from each team to solve cross team issues quickly.

Risk 4

Equipment may arrive late or not work properly

Coffee machines, grinders, POS systems, refrigerators, and other tools are essential for opening the stores. If equipment delivery is late, the store cannot start training or soft opening on time. If equipment has technical problems, drink preparation and payment service may be affected.

This risk affects schedule because the store opening may need to be delayed. It affects cost because repair, replacement, or urgent shipping can be expensive. It also affects quality because staff cannot serve customers well without stable equipment.

The mitigation plan is to order equipment early and choose suppliers with clear warranty and support service. The Operations team should check all equipment before the soft opening. The contingency plan is to keep backup equipment for important items, arrange supplier technicians on call, and transfer equipment from another store if necessary.

Risk 5

Opening campaigns may attract customers only for a short time

A grand opening campaign can bring many customers at first, but customers may stop coming after the discount period ends. This can happen if customers only come for promotions or if the store does not create a strong experience. If repeat customers are low, the store will struggle after the first month.

This risk affects revenue and long term store performance. It can also increase marketing cost because the company may need to run more promotions. Too many discounts may also reduce the premium image of the brand.

The mitigation plan is to focus not only on discounts but also on customer experience. The store should provide good drinks, friendly service, comfortable space, and loyalty programs. The contingency plan is to collect customer information, invite customers to join membership programs, create returning customer offers, and improve service based on feedback.

Risk 6

Operating standards may be different between stores

Because the project opens stores in five different locations, each store may operate differently if standards are not clear. One store may have faster service, while another store may have weaker hygiene or different drink taste. This can make the brand inconsistent.

This risk affects quality and customer trust. If customers have different experiences in different stores, they may think the brand is not professional. It can also affect cost because the company may need to fix mistakes after opening.

The mitigation plan is to prepare standard operating procedures for all stores. These procedures should include recipes, cleaning rules, service steps, inventory control, cash handling, and complaint handling. The contingency plan is to send an operations audit team to check stores regularly and retrain teams that do not meet standards.
