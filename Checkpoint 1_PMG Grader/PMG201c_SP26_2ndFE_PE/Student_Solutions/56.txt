Request 1:

Project name:
Coi Cafe Central Pilot Flagship Project

Justifications:
Open five pilot flagship stores in Da Nang, Hue, Quang Nam, Quang Ngai, and Binh Dinh.
Use the project to test Coi Cafe’s ability to operate outside Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City.
Build a controlled expansion model before scaling to more provinces in the future.
Introduce Coi Cafe’s traditional Vietnamese drinks, nostalgic space, and sustainable practices to Central customers.
Create a stronger supply connection between Dak Lak coffee farmers and Central Vietnam stores.
Increase brand visibility in tourism and local community areas.
Generate new revenue and ensure each store reaches break-even within eight months after opening.

Project constraints:
Scope:
Open only five flagship stores, one in each assigned province.
Project scope includes: feasibility study, location selection, store design, construction, equipment setup, staff hiring, training, supply chain setup, marketing launch, and performance tracking.
The project does not include franchising, online delivery expansion, or opening more than five stores.
Time:
Total project duration is 12 months.
All stores must be opened within 12 months.
Each store must reach break-even within eight months after opening.
Cost / Budget:
Total budget is VND 8.5 billion.
Average budget is approximately VND 1.7 billion per store.
Budget must be controlled because it is funded by equity and commercial bank loans.
Quality:
All stores must maintain Coi Cafe’s brand identity, drink quality, service standards, and sustainable operating model.
Each store must be ready for stable daily operation before official opening.

Request 2:

Item 1: Feasibility study and local market validation
Name: Market feasibility study
Description: Cost for analyzing demand, competitors, customer profile, expected revenue, and suitable store concept in each province.
Estimation: VND 700 million total, about VND 140 million per store.
Way to estimate: Based on field research cost, customer interviews, competitor visits, travel expenses, and local data collection.
Person in charge: Project Manager and Marketing Team.

Item 2: Premises leasing and legal preparation
Name: Leasing and legal setup
Description: Cost for premises search, lease negotiation, deposit, first rent payment, legal review, and store permit preparation.
Estimation: VND 1.5 billion total, about VND 300 million per store.
Way to estimate: Based on rental market price, deposit conditions, legal service fee, and broker fee.
Person in charge: Project Manager and Finance Team.

Item 3: Standardized store build-out
Name: Store build-out and brand setup
Description: Cost for construction, furniture, lighting, signage, counter, seating area, decoration, and brand-standard store finishing.
Estimation: VND 3.0 billion total, about VND 600 million per store.
Way to estimate: Based on approved design template, store size, construction quotation, furniture list, and material price.
Person in charge: Interior Design and Construction Team.

Item 4: Operating equipment and digital systems
Name: Equipment and digital operation system
Description: Cost for coffee machines, grinders, refrigerators, POS system, CCTV, Wi-Fi, water filters, reusable cups, and kitchen tools.
Estimation: VND 1.25 billion total, about VND 250 million per store.
Way to estimate: Based on standard equipment package and quotations from approved suppliers.
Person in charge: Procurement and Operations Team.

Item 5: Staffing and opening readiness
Name: Staff recruitment and opening training
Description: Cost for recruitment, interviewing, onboarding, uniforms, training materials, trainer travel, and pre-opening salary.
Estimation: VND 800 million total, about VND 160 million per store.
Way to estimate: Based on required staff number, hiring channels, training schedule, salary level, and uniform cost.
Person in charge: HR Team.

Item 6: Supply chain launch package
Name: Raw material and logistics setup
Description: Cost for first inventory, supplier qualification, delivery route setup, storage preparation, quality testing, and safety stock.
Estimation: VND 1.0 billion total, about VND 200 million per store.
Way to estimate: Based on opening inventory list, supplier price, transportation cost, and expected first-month consumption.
Person in charge: Procurement and Supply Chain Team.

Item 7: Brand activation and contingency
Name: Launch activation and risk reserve
Description: Cost for grand opening promotion, local advertising, community activation, trial vouchers, and reserve for unexpected project issues.
Estimation: VND 1.25 billion total, about VND 250 million per store.
Way to estimate: Based on campaign scale, media cost, event cost, promotion value, and expected risk level.
Person in charge: Marketing Team and Project Manager.

Request 3: 
Risk1: Pilot model does not fit all provinces 
Risk name: One standard store model may not match different local markets
Decription: Customer behavior, tourism flow, income level, and coffee habits may vary between Da Nang, Hue, Quang Nam, Quang Ngai, and Binh Dinh
Impacts:
Scope / quality: Store concept may not satisfy local customers in some provinces.
Time: More time may be needed to adjust menu, pricing, or marketing.
Cost: Extra spending for research, redesign, or promotion.
Mitigation plan: Conduct market validation for each province and allow small local adjustments while keeping the main brand concept.
Contingency plan: Revise menu bundles, pricing, or store communication for weaker provinces after soft opening feedback.

Risk 2: Cash flow pressure during simultaneous expansion
Risk name: Budget pressure from opening five stores within one year.
Description: Construction, equipment, rent, training, and marketing payments may happen close together, creating cash flow pressure.
Impacts:
Scope / quality: Some non-essential items may need to be delayed or reduced.
Time: Payment delays may slow construction or supplier delivery.
Cost: Loan interest and emergency funding costs may increase.
Mitigation plan: Prepare a monthly cash flow plan, approve spending by phase, and monitor actual cost against budget.
Contingency plan: Open stores in phases and prioritize stores with stronger expected revenue if cash flow becomes tight.

Risk 3: Quality control failure after launch
Risk name: New stores cannot maintain consistent operation quality.
Description: New employees and new suppliers may cause inconsistency in drink taste, service, hygiene, or reusable cup practice.
Impacts:
Scope / quality: Brand reputation may be damaged.
Time: Additional training and quality checks may be required.
Cost: Extra cost for retraining, replacement materials, and customer recovery actions.
Mitigation plan: Use standard operating procedures, pre-opening quality checklist, and supervisor inspection before launch.
Contingency plan: Assign experienced staff from existing stores to support new branches and correct quality issues immediately.
