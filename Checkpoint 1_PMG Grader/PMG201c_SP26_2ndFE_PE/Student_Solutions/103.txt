Request 1:
Project name:
The project is titled “Coi Cafe Central Expansion Project 2026”, implemented by Coi Cafe to expand its business operations into five Central provinces including Da Nang, Hue, Quang Nam, Quang Ngai, and Binh Dinh.
Justification (Purpose & Reasons):
The project aims to expand Coi Cafe’s market presence outside Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City while increasing brand awareness and revenue growth. The expansion is implemented to respond to the growing demand for Vietnamese traditional coffee spaces and sustainable coffee products in Central Vietnam. In addition, the project supports the company’s long-term objective of becoming a nationwide coffee chain while maintaining its environmentally friendly business model through reusable cups and direct sourcing from Dak Lak farmers.
Project constraints:
In terms of scope, the project includes surveying and selecting store locations, designing and constructing interiors, recruiting and training local employees, building supply chain partnerships, and implementing launch marketing campaigns for five flagship stores. In terms of time, the project must be completed within 12 months with multiple activities implemented simultaneously. Regarding costs, the total budget is limited to 8.5 billion VND funded by company equity and bank loans, requiring strict budget control. In terms of quality, all stores must follow the same brand identity and customer service standards while achieving the break-even point within 8 months after opening.

Request 2:
Cost Item 1:
The first cost item is store rental and deposit expenses for five flagship locations in Central provinces. The estimated budget is approximately 2 billion VND. This estimation is based on average commercial rental prices and location requirements analyzed by the Market Development team.
Cost Item 2:
The second cost item is interior design and construction. This includes renovation work, furniture, lighting systems, decorations, and branding materials for each store. The estimated cost is around 2.5 billion VND. The Interior Design team calculates this budget using contractor quotations and previous project data.
Cost Item 3:
The third cost item is recruitment and training activities for local employees, baristas, and store managers. The estimated budget is approximately 800 million VND. This amount is estimated by the HR department based on staffing requirements, training materials, and salary standards.
Cost Item 4:
The fourth cost item is marketing and launch campaigns. This includes social media advertising, opening events, promotional activities, and printed marketing materials. The estimated cost is around 1 billion VND. The Marketing team estimates this budget using historical campaign performance and projected promotional needs.
Cost Item 5:
The fifth cost item is supply chain setup and equipment investment. This includes coffee machines, kitchen equipment, logistics arrangements, and supplier contracts. The estimated budget is approximately 1.5 billion VND. This estimation is prepared through quotations from suppliers and logistics partners.

Request 3:
Risk 1 – Construction Delay:
Construction and interior setup activities may be delayed due to contractor issues, material shortages, or unexpected weather conditions. This risk may affect the project timeline and increase labor or rental costs. To reduce this risk, the project team will regularly monitor contractor performance and construction progress. In case delays occur, backup contractors and alternative suppliers will be prepared as contingency solutions.
Risk 2 – Low Customer Demand:
The new stores may not attract enough customers during the early stages of operation because the brand is entering new regional markets. This risk may reduce revenue growth and delay the break-even target. To mitigate this risk, the company will conduct market research and implement localized marketing campaigns before the store launch. As a contingency plan, the company may introduce discounts, loyalty programs, and promotional events to increase customer engagement.
Risk 3 – Supply Chain Disruption:
Suppliers in Central provinces may fail to deliver materials and products on time due to transportation or operational issues. This risk may interrupt store operations and increase logistics costs. To minimize this risk, the company will cooperate with multiple suppliers and establish long-term agreements with reliable partners. If disruptions occur, temporary suppliers from nearby provinces will be used to maintain operations.

Request 4:
Activity 1 – Project Planning:
For project planning activities, the Project Manager is both responsible and accountable for preparing the project schedule, budget, and implementation strategy. The Interior Design Team is consulted regarding store concepts, while the HR Team and Marketing Team are informed about project objectives and timelines.
Activity 2 – Site Selection:
For site selection activities, the Project Manager is responsible and accountable for final location decisions. The Interior Design Team and Marketing Team are consulted to evaluate store layout suitability and market potential, while the HR Team is informed about the selected locations.
Activity 3 – Interior Design and Construction:
For interior design and construction activities, the Interior Design Team is responsible for creating store concepts and supervising construction work. The Project Manager is accountable for approving progress and ensuring that the work meets project standards. The HR Team and Marketing Team are informed about construction updates.
Activity 4 – Recruitment and Training:
For recruitment and training activities, the HR Team is responsible and accountable for hiring employees and organizing training programs. The Project Manager is consulted regarding staffing requirements, while the Marketing Team is consulted for customer service training support.
Activity 5 – Marketing Campaign Planning:
For marketing campaign planning, the Marketing Team is responsible and accountable for advertising activities, promotional strategies, and grand opening events. The Project Manager is consulted to ensure that campaigns align with project goals, while the Interior Design Team and HR Team are informed about campaign schedules and branding requirements.
Activity 6 – Supplier Selection:
For supplier selection activities, the Project Manager is responsible and accountable for selecting logistics and raw material suppliers. The Interior Design Team is consulted regarding equipment quality and store requirements, while the HR Team and Marketing Team are informed about supplier arrangements.
Activity 7 – Grand Opening Event:
For the grand opening event, the Marketing Team is responsible for organizing promotional activities and attracting customers. The Project Manager is accountable for approving the final event plan. The HR Team is consulted regarding staff preparation, while the Interior Design Team is informed about the opening schedule.
