Request 1 (20%) – Project Charter
Project Name:
Coi Cafe Strategic Expansion Project – Central Vietnam 2026
Justification (Purpose & Reasons)
This project is designed to expand Coi Cafe’s operations into five Central provinces: Da Nang, Hue, Quang Nam, Quang Ngai, and Binh Dinh. These areas show strong economic growth, increasing urbanization, and a rising middle class with higher demand for premium café experiences.
The expansion will allow Coi Cafe to capture new customer segments, increase brand awareness across regions, and enhance its competitive advantage. Additionally, the project supports long-term business sustainability by integrating local sourcing strategies and environmentally friendly operations.
Project Constraints
Scope:
 The project includes establishing 5 new flagship stores with consistent branding, operational systems, and customer service standards. It also involves staff recruitment, training, and supplier coordination.
Time:
 The project must be completed within 12 months, and each store is expected to reach break-even within 6–8 months after opening.
Cost/Budget:
 The total budget is estimated at VND 8.5 billion, averaging VND 1.6–1.7 billion per store, funded by a combination of internal capital and bank loans.
Quality:
 All stores must meet company standards in design, product quality, service experience, and sustainability. Employees must complete training before operations begin.

2)
Request 3 (30%) – Risk Management
Risk 1: Delayed Project Execution
Description: Delays due to construction issues or poor coordination
Impact: Increased costs and missed deadlines
Mitigation: Strong project planning and regular monitoring
Contingency: Add schedule buffer and adjust timeline

Risk 2: Weak Customer Demand
Description: New stores fail to attract expected customers
Impact: Low revenue and delayed profitability
Mitigation: Conduct market analysis and choose strategic locations
Contingency: Increase marketing efforts and improve product offerings

Risk 3: Supply Chain Inefficiency
Description: Disruptions in material supply or inconsistent quality
Impact: Operational interruptions and customer dissatisfaction
Mitigation: Work with reliable suppliers and monitor quality
Contingency: Maintain backup suppliers and inventory reserves
