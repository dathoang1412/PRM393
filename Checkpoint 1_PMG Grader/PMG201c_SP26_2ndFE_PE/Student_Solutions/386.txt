Objective 1:

Complete the opening of 5 Coi Cafe flagship stores in Central Vietnam within 12 months.

Specific: Open one flagship store in each selected province, including Da Nang, Hue, Quang Nam, Quang Ngai, and Binh Dinh.
Measurable: Track the number of stores that are fully constructed, equipped, staffed, and officially opened.
Achievable: Realistic because Coi Cafe already has 12 stores and has experience in store management and customer service.
Relevant: Supports the company’s expansion strategy and helps the brand enter new regional markets.
Time-bound: Must be completed within 12 months from the project start date.
Objective 2:

Set up a reliable local supply chain and staffing system for all 5 new stores before launch.

Specific: Recruit and train local employees, and connect with Central region suppliers for coffee and other materials.
Measurable: Measure through signed supplier agreements, completed training results, and readiness checklists for each store.
Achievable: Possible because the company can combine its existing operation standards with local supplier networks.
Relevant: Important for keeping product quality stable and helping stores operate without major interruption.
Time-bound: Must be ready before each store officially opens.

Request 3: Define at least 5 quality assurance (QA) or quality control (QC) activities for the project
1. Market Survey Review (QA):

Conducted in the early stage to make sure the team understands local customer demand, competitors, rental levels, and suitable store areas. This helps the project avoid choosing locations based only on personal judgment.

2. Location Approval Check (QA):

Before signing any rental contract, the project manager and management team review the selected premises. They check visibility, customer traffic, parking, rental cost, and whether the location fits Coi Cafe’s target customers.

3. Design Consistency Review (QA):

Performed before construction begins. The interior design team and project manager review whether the layout, colors, furniture, and decoration still reflect Coi’s nostalgic Vietnamese coffee style.

4. Construction Quality Inspection (QC):

Performed regularly during the construction stage. The team checks materials, finishing quality, electrical work, lighting, seating layout, and safety conditions to make sure the store is built correctly.

5. Supplier Quality Testing (QC):

Before choosing suppliers, the team tests coffee beans and other materials, and checks delivery reliability. This helps ensure the stores can maintain stable drink quality after opening.

6. Staff Training Evaluation (QC):

After training sessions, employees are tested on drink preparation, customer service, POS usage, and basic store procedures. This ensures that new staff can work properly before the opening day.

7. Final Pre-opening Checklist (QC):

Before the official launch, the team checks store cleanliness, equipment function, menu readiness, staff readiness, and customer service flow. This reduces operational problems during the first few days.
