Request 1:

Project name:
Coi Cafe Central Operations Control Expansion Project

Justifications:
Expand Coi Cafe into five Central provinces: Da Nang, Hue, Quang Nam, Quang Ngai, and Binh Dinh.
Use a strong operations control model to ensure all new stores are opened on time, within budget, and with consistent quality.
Standardize store setup, staff training, supplier selection, inventory control, and launch execution across all five provinces.
Maintain Coi Cafe’s nostalgic Vietnamese coffee space, traditional drink menu, reusable cup practice, and direct sourcing from Dak Lak farmers.
Reduce operational risks caused by managing multiple new stores in different provinces.
Build a controlled regional expansion system that can be reused for future markets.
Create new revenue from five stores and support the break-even target within eight months after opening.

Project constraints:
Scope:
Open five flagship stores only, one store in each selected province.
Project scope includes: market survey, premises selection, lease agreement, design approval, construction, equipment setup, staff recruitment, training, supplier setup, launch campaign, operation checklist, and performance reporting.
The project does not include franchising, extra stores, new product development, or expansion outside the five selected provinces.
Time:
The project must be completed within 12 months.
All five stores must be opened within the approved schedule.
Operational control checklists must be completed before each store launch.
Each store must reach break-even within eight months after opening.
Cost / Budget:
Total budget is VND 8.5 billion.
Average budget is about VND 1.7 billion per store.
Funding comes from equity and commercial bank loans.
Spending must be controlled through budget tracking and approval checkpoints.
Quality:
All stores must meet Coi Cafe’s standard operating requirements.
Interior design, drink recipes, service process, hygiene, sustainability practice, and supplier quality must be consistent.
Each store must pass final operation readiness review before grand opening.

Description: Cost for checking customer demand, competitor situation, rental conditions, sales potential, and operational difficulty level in each province.
Estimation: VND 650 million total, around VND 130 million per store.

Name: Premises selection and lease control

Estimation: VND 1.5 billion total, around VND 300 million per store.
Way to estimate: Based on rental market price, deposit terms, broker fee, legal checking fee, and first rental payment.

Name: Construction and interior setup under brand control
Description: Cost for construction, furniture, lighting, service counter, seating area, decoration, signage, and required brand-standard finishing.

Request 3:

Risk 1: Control process slows down execution
Risk name: Too many approvals delay store opening.
Description: A strict operations control model may require many reviews and approvals, causing delays in construction, purchasing, or launch preparation.
Impacts:
Scope / quality: Some teams may rush final work to meet deadlines.
Time: Store opening dates may be delayed.
Cost: Extra rent, labor, and contractor waiting costs may occur.
Mitigation plan: Define clear approval levels and deadlines for each decision before project execution starts.
Contingency plan: Allow the Project Manager to approve urgent operational decisions within budget limits.

Risk 2: Teams do not follow standard procedures
Risk name: Poor compliance with operatineg standards.
Description: Local teams, contractors, or suppliers may skip checklists or fail to follow Coi Cafe’s standard process.
Impacts:
Scope / quality: Store quality, drink taste, or service consistency may be reduced.
Time: Rework or retraining may delay launch.
Cost: Correction cost, supervision cost, or replacement cost may increase.
Mitigation plan: Use mandatory checklists, weekly inspections, and clear acceptance criteria before payment or store opening.
Contingency plan: Stop launch for stores that fail critical standards and assign a support team to correct issues.

Risk 3: Performance monitoring shows weak early sales
Risk name: Early sales below planned break-even path.
Description: After opening, performance reports may show that customer traffic and sales are lower than the planned break-even target.
Impacts:
Scope / quality: The store may not deliver the expected business result.
Time: Break-even may exceed eight months.
Cost: Extra marketing, promotion, or operating support may be required.
Mitigation plan: Monitor daily sales, customer counts, and feedback during the first month after opening.
Contingency plan: Activate a recovery plan with local promotions, cost control, menu bundle adjustment, and additional marketing support.
