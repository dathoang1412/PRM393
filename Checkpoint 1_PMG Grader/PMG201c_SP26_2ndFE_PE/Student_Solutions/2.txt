Request 1 Narrative Charter Statement

Project name: Coi Cafe Five Province Growth Project in Central Vietnam.

Justifications: Coi Cafe currently operates 12 stores in Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City. The company wants to expand its Vietnamese coffee concept to five Central provinces: Da Nang, Hue, Quang Nam, Quang Ngai, and Binh Dinh. The project will create one flagship store in each province within 12 months.

The project is necessary because Coi Cafe needs to reduce dependence on two large cities and enter new markets with tourism and local customer potential. The Central region can help the brand reach both domestic tourists and young local consumers. The project also strengthens Coi Cafe’s sustainable positioning by continuing direct sourcing from Dak Lak farmers and using reusable cups in all new stores.

Project constraints: The scope is fixed at five flagship stores. The project includes location selection, lease preparation, store design, construction, equipment purchasing, hiring, training, supplier setup, and launch marketing. The project does not include opening franchise stores or changing the main menu.

The schedule constraint is 12 months. Each store must break even within 8 months after opening. The budget constraint is VND 8.5 billion in total, using company equity and commercial bank loans. The quality constraint is that each store must follow the same nostalgic style, drink quality, sustainability policy, and customer experience as existing Coi Cafe stores.

Request 2 Main Cost and Budget Items
Market research and leasing: This includes customer analysis, competitor review, location visits, lease negotiation, legal checking, and deposits. Estimated cost is VND 650 million. The method is market comparison. Person in charge is the Market Development Manager.
Store design and construction: This includes architectural design, interior fit-out, decoration, lighting, counter area, and construction labor. Estimated cost is VND 3.7 billion. The method is parametric estimation based on fit-out cost per store. Person in charge is the Construction Manager.
Store equipment and furniture: This includes coffee machines, storage equipment, POS system, tables, chairs, reusable cups, and dishwashing equipment. Estimated cost is VND 1.3 billion. The method is bottom-up estimation from supplier quotations. Person in charge is the Procurement Team.
Human resource setup: This includes hiring and training store managers, baristas, cashiers, and service staff. Estimated cost is VND 800 million. The method is salary and training cost estimation. Person in charge is the HR Manager.
Supply chain setup: This includes logistics, supplier onboarding, purchase of initial raw materials, and coordination with Dak Lak coffee suppliers. Estimated cost is VND 550 million. The method is bottom-up estimation. Person in charge is the Supply Chain Team.
Opening marketing: This includes local advertising, soft opening, grand opening, promotion vouchers, and influencer posts. Estimated cost is VND 550 million. The method is percentage and analogous estimation. Person in charge is the Marketing Manager.
Project management and legal administration: This includes reporting, travel, permits, bank loan documents, and coordination. Estimated cost is VND 350 million. The method is analogous estimation. Person in charge is the Project Manager.
Contingency reserve: This covers unexpected cost increases and risk responses. Estimated cost is VND 600 million. The method is risk reserve estimation. Person in charge is Finance Team.

Total estimated budget: VND 8.5 billion.

Request 3 Three Project Risks

Risk 1 High lease cost: Good locations in city centers may be more expensive than expected. This can affect scope and quality if the team chooses smaller or less suitable stores. It can also delay lease signing and increase costs by VND 200 million to VND 400 million. Mitigation is to compare several locations before signing. Contingency is to select second-best locations with lower rent but acceptable traffic.

Risk 2 Supply disruption: Coffee beans and other materials may arrive late because the project serves five provinces at the same time. This can reduce product quality, delay opening preparation, and increase logistics cost by VND 100 million to VND 250 million. Mitigation is to sign contracts early and keep safety stock. Contingency is to use approved backup suppliers.

Risk 3 Weak launch performance: Customers may not know Coi Cafe in new provinces. This can reduce sales quality, slow break-even progress, and require extra marketing costs of VND 200 million to VND 350 million. Mitigation is to run local campaigns before opening. Contingency is to add promotions, cooperate with local communities, and adjust advertising channels.
