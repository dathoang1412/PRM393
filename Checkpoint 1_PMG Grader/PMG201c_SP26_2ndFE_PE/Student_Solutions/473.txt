Request 1:
Project name:
Coi Cafe Central Destination Store Network Project
Justifications:
Expand Coi Cafe into five Central provinces: Da Nang, Hue, Quang am, Quang Ngai, and Binh Dinh.
Build each flagship store as a “destination café” where customers intentioally visit for atmosphere, Vietnamese coffee, and brand experience.
Create memorable stores that combine Coi Cafe’s nostalgic space, traditional drinks, reusable cup practice, and sustainable sourcing message.
Increase customer traffic by choosing locations that are easy to access for local residents, travelers, students, and office workers.
Strengthen Coi Cafe’s brand visibility in Central Vietnam through five strong flagship locations instead of small ordinary stores.
Build a regional supplier and operating network to support daily store performance.
Generate new revenue and ensure each store reaches break-even within eight months after opening.
Project constraints:
Scope:
Open five destination-style flagship stores only, one store in each selected province.
Project scope includes: destination location survey, premises selection, lease agreement, store design, construction, equipment setup, recruitment, training, supply chain setup, launch campaign, and performance monitoring.
The project does not include franchising, extra stores, delivery-only stores, or unrelated tourism services.
Time:
The total project duration is 12 months.
All e stores must be opened within the approed timeline.
Each store must reach break-even within eight months after opening.
Cost / Budget:
Total budget is VND 8.5 billion.
Average budget is approximately VND 1.7 billion per store.
Funding comes from company equity and commercial bank loans.
Costs must be controlled, especially for location rental, construction, equipment, marketing, and staff preparation.
Quality:
All stores must follow Coi Cafe’s flagship quality standards.
Store design must be nostalgic, comfortable, recognizable, and suitable for destination visits.
Drink quality, service quality, hygiene, and sustainable practices must be consistent.
Each store must be fully ready before official opening.

Description: Cost for studying high-accessibility areas, customer movement, competitor cafés, parking convenience, visibility, and destination potential in each province.

Request 3:
Risk 1: Destination store does not attract intentional visits
Risk name: Customers do not see the store as a destination.
Description: The store may be attractive but not strong enough to make customers intentionally visit, especially if the location is not convenient or the experience is not unique.
Impacts:
Scope / quality: The store may perform like an ordinary café instead of a flagship destination.
Time: More time may be needed to build customer habits and awareness.
Cost: Extra marketing and promotion cost may be needed.
Mitigation plan: Select locations with strong accessibility, create memorable interior features, and promote the store experience before opening.
Contingency plan: Add local events, photo campaigns, group promotions, and limited-time offers to increase visits.
Risk 2: High location cost reduces profitability
Risk name: Destination locations create high fixed costs.
Description: Accessible or high-visibility premises may require higher rent, deposit, and renovation cost.
Impacts:
Scope / quality: Store size or design features may need to be reduced.
Time: Lease negotiation may take longer.
Cost: Higher fixed cost may delay break-even beyond eight months.
Mitigation plan: Set rental cost limits, compare multiple locations, and calculate break-even before signing lease.
Contingency plan: Select a nearby lower-cost location and increase local marketing to compensate for lower visibility.
Risk 3: Customer traffic is unstable after opening
Risk name: Initial traffic drops after launch period.
Description: Customers may visit during grand opening but not return regularly if the store does not build repeat value.
Impacts:
Scope / quality: The store may not maintain strong brand presence.
Time: Break-even progress may slow down.
Cost: Additional customer retention campaigns may be required.
Mitigation plan: Build loyalty programs, collect customer feedback, and create repeat-visit offers after launch.
Contingency plan: Adjust menu bundles, launch membership campaigns, cooperate with nearby offices and schools, and improve service quality.
