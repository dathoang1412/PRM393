Request 1 – Project Charter
Project Name:
Coi Cafe Mobile & Event Activation Project – Central Vietnam 2026
Justification (Purpose & Reasons):
This project introduces a mobile café and event-based activation strategy, where Coi Cafe operates coffee trucks and temporary pop-up booths at high-traffic events such as festivals, concerts, universities, and tourist areas across Central Vietnam.
Instead of investing heavily in fixed locations, this approach allows the brand to:
Reach diverse customer segments through on-demand locations
Test multiple markets quickly with minimal long-term commitment
Increase brand visibility through event participation and experiential marketing
Generate flexible revenue streams based on seasonal demand
This model supports agile expansion and enhances brand exposure while reducing the risk associated with permanent store investments.
Project Constraints
Scope:
Deploy 3 mobile coffee trucks and 2 portable pop-up booths
Participate in at least 15 major events across Central Vietnam
Develop a flexible menu suitable for mobile operations
Coordinate logistics, permits, and event partnerships
Time:
Total duration: 12 months
Vehicle setup & preparation: 3 months
Event operations: 7 months
Evaluation & scaling: 2 months
Break-even target: within 6–7 months
Cost/Budget:
Total budget: VND 8.5 billion
Allocation:
Coffee trucks & equipment: ~4 billion
Pop-up booth setup: ~1 billion
Logistics & transportation: ~1.5 billion
Marketing & event participation: ~2 billion
Funding from internal capital and event sponsorships
Quality:
Maintain consistent product quality across all mobile units
Ensure fast service speed during peak event hours
Maintain hygiene and safety standards in mobile operations
Customer satisfaction target ≥ 90%

2)
Request 3 – Risk Management
Risk 1: Unpredictable Event Attendance
Description: Low turnout at events affects sales
Impact: Revenue fluctuation
Mitigation: Select high-profile events and diversify locations
Contingency: Shift to alternative high-traffic areas (tourist spots, campuses)
Risk 2: Weather Conditions
Description: Rain or extreme heat affects operations
Impact: Reduced customer demand and operational disruption
Mitigation: Use weather-resistant equipment and tents
Contingency: Reschedule or relocate to indoor events
Risk 3: Operational Complexity
Description: Managing multiple mobile units across locations
Impact: Inefficiency and coordination issues
Mitigation: Use centralized scheduling and tracking system
Contingency: Reduce number of active units temporarily
