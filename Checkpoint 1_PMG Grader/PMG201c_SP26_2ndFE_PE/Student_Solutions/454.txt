Request 1: 
1. Project Name: Coi cafe - The central expansion project (Da Nang, Hue, Quang Nam, Quang Ngai, Binh Dinh).
2. Justifications: Coi Cafe is already famous in Hanoi and Ho chi minh city for its nostalgic style and sustainable business. We want to implement this project to expand our brand to 5 central provinces. We choose Da Nang, Hue, Quang Nam, Quang Ngai, and Binh Dinh because these are high potential markets where people appreciate the traditional culture. By building a supply chain (flagship stores) with central region suppliers, we can reduce costs and support local farmers, just like we did with our Dak Lak partners. This project will help the brand grow larger and more professional by opening one flagship store in each of these provinces.
3. Project Constraints:
- Scope: We only open 5 stores, one in each province (Da Nang, Hue, Quang Nam, Quang Ngai, Binh Dinh). The project include finding the place, designing, hiring people, marketing and selling our drinks.
- Time: The whole project must be finished in 12 months (milestone: from 1 June 2026 to 1 June 2027)
- Cost/budget: The total budget is VND 8.5 billion (around 1.7 billion per store). This money come from our own equity and from the bank loans.
- Quality: Every store must have the same nostalgic style and use sustainable materials like reusable cups, and use local natural ingredients.
- Success factor: Each store need to reach the break even point within 8 months after it starts operating.

Request 2: 
1. Cost to rent location for store rental and deposit: This is the money to rent 5 buildings for our 5 flagship stores. It is about VND 1.6 billion. The market development director (PM) will estimate this by looking at the house prices in the the city centers of Da Nang,Hue,… and it depends on locations, deals and time PM decide to rent.
2. Interior and construction: This budget is for building the shop and buying furniture to make it look old-style and have noslical vibe. It costs around VND 3.7 billion. The interior team leader will estimate the cost after they finish the design decorations for each store.
3. Human resource and training workers: This cost is for hiring local staff and teaching them how to make coffee, drinks, clean stores,…. It is about VND 800 million. The HR team will calculate this based on the number of workers we need for 5 flagship shops.
4. Material supply chain setup: This include the cost to find and work with local coffee and other ingrdients suppliers in 5 central provinces. It is about VND 1.1 billion. The PM will be in charge of this by visiting the farms and checking the prices and make the deals with the local suppliers.
5. Launching and marketing: This is for the opening events, granding opening and ads on Facebook, tiktok, instagram. We estimate it costs VND 1.3 billion. The marketing team will prepare this by asking for prices from local event companies.

Request 3: 
1. Risk of slow Construction because of 5 province’s special weather: In the central region of vietnam, espacially 5 provines we ready to open store, there are many rains and storms, most in the middle and the last of the year. This can make the construction late, delate of time, and maybe increase the labor cost, lost of cost of buiding and working. To fix this, we will check the weather forecast carefully and have a backup team of workers to finish the job faster when the weather is good (sunny).
2. Risk of local suppliers dont meet our brands’ standards: Sometimes, local farmers might not have enough high-quality coffee/tea/other local ingredients like fruits,… for us. This will affect the drink quality, lower the customers using drinks quality. We will solve this by signing contracts with many different suppliers and check the materials every month. And build a list of at least 3 suppliers per ingredients to avoid the risk of lacking high quality ingredients.
3. Risk of hard to reach the break-even point: Maybe the local people dont like the nostalgic style, or simple dont fit with our drink’s tastes, so we cannot make profit in 8 months. This will impact our cost and the bank loan. To response this, the marketing team will make special drinks that fit the local taste, try to make and test drinks in term of can fit the locals taste, and have big promotions to attract more people, like sale for students (potential customers) with fruits drinks/boba, sale for workers with coffee, sales in holidays, in granding opening weekens make buy 2 get 1 programs, vouchers for customers who usually using our drinks,…


Request 4: 
1. Project roles:
- Project Manager (PM): The person who leads the whole project in each province.
- Marketing team leader: The person in charge of ads and opening events and promotion programs,…
- HR Team Leader: The person in charge of hiring and training workers,… and HR operating activities

2. Tasks and responsibilities:
- Surveying and choosing premises: The PM is responsible (R) for finding the places. The other two leaders are informed (I) about the final decision.
- Managing the 8.5 billion budget: The PM is accountable (A) and responsible (R) for all the money.
- Designing the store interior: The PM is accountable for the design (A). The Marketing team is consulted (C) to make sure the store looks good for taking photos, videos,….
- Building and decoration: The PM is responsible (R) for working with the construction team. The HR team is informed (I) about when the store is ready.
- Finding local suppliers in central regions: The PM is responsible (R) for the supply chain. The marketing team is consulted (C)for the brand story.
- Hiring local baristas, workers and managers: The HR team leader is responsible (R) for recruiting. The PM is accountable (A) for the final choice.
- Training staff about Coi Cafe style: The HR team leader is responsible (R) for the training sessions. The PM is informed (I).
- Planning marketing campaigns: The marketing team lleader is responsible (R) for the ads. The PM is accountable (A) and must approve the plan.
- Organizing the opening day: The marketing team leader is responsible (R) for the event. The HR team is consulted to prepare the staff (C)
- Checking the 8-month break-even goal: The PM is responsible (R) for checking the revenue every month. The marketing team is informed (I) so they can change the ads if needed.
