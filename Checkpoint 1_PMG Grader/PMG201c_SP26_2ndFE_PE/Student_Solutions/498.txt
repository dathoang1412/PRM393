REQUEST 1:
Project name:
The project is called “Coi Cafe Regional Development Project 2026”, organized by Coi Cafe to establish five new flagship stores in Central Vietnam, including Da Nang, Hue, Quang Nam, Quang Ngai, and Binh Dinh.
Justification (Purpose & Reasons):
The purpose of the project is to expand Coi Cafe’s business network and strengthen its competitive position in the Vietnamese coffee market. The company recognizes the increasing popularity of traditional coffee culture and sustainable lifestyle trends among young consumers in Central Vietnam. Therefore, the expansion project is expected to attract more customers, increase long-term revenue, and improve brand recognition nationwide. Moreover, the project helps Coi Cafe maintain its commitment to sustainability through reusable products and ethically sourced coffee beans from local farmers.
Project constraints:
Regarding scope, the project includes location research, store construction and decoration, employee recruitment and training, supplier coordination, and promotional activities for five stores. In terms of time, the implementation period is limited to 12 months, requiring close coordination between departments. For cost constraints, the project budget is capped at 8.5 billion VND and must be carefully managed because the funding comes from both company capital and bank loans. Concerning quality, all stores must provide consistent service quality, maintain the company’s nostalgic design concept, and achieve operational break-even within 8 months.

REQUEST 2:
Cost Item 1:
The first major cost item is store leasing and deposit payments for prime commercial locations in Central provinces. The estimated cost for this category is around 2 billion VND. The estimation is developed by the Market Development department based on rental surveys and property evaluations.
Cost Item 2:
The second cost item is store renovation and interior decoration. This includes construction materials, furniture, lighting equipment, signage, and branding design. The estimated budget is approximately 2.5 billion VND. The Interior Design team prepares this estimate using quotations from contractors and suppliers.
Cost Item 3:
The third cost item involves recruitment and staff training programs. Expenses include recruitment advertisements, onboarding activities, training workshops, and employee salaries during the preparation phase. The estimated budget is about 800 million VND, calculated by the HR department according to staffing plans.
Cost Item 4:
The fourth cost item is promotional and marketing activities for store launches. This budget covers digital advertising, opening ceremonies, social media campaigns, and printed promotional materials. The estimated cost is around 1 billion VND. The Marketing department estimates this amount using previous campaign performance data.
Cost Item 5:
The fifth cost item is equipment purchasing and supply chain development. This includes coffee machines, kitchen appliances, transportation arrangements, and supplier contracts for raw materials. The estimated budget is approximately 1.5 billion VND based on quotations from logistics providers and equipment suppliers.

REQUEST 3:
Risk 1 – Delays in Construction Progress:
One possible risk is delays in construction and store preparation due to contractor performance issues or shortages of materials. This problem may postpone store opening schedules and increase operational expenses. To minimize the risk, the company will supervise construction progress regularly and maintain communication with contractors. As a contingency plan, backup contractors and replacement suppliers will be prepared if necessary.
Risk 2 – Weak Customer Response:
Another project risk is low customer interest after the stores begin operating in new regional markets. This situation may negatively affect sales performance and delay the break-even target. To reduce this risk, the company plans to conduct customer research and strengthen local marketing activities before launch. If customer traffic remains low, additional promotional programs and discount campaigns will be introduced.
Risk 3 – Supplier and Logistics Problems:
Supply chain disruptions may occur because of transportation delays or supplier operational failures. This risk may interrupt store activities and increase logistics costs. To mitigate this issue, the company will cooperate with multiple suppliers and establish backup supply agreements. If disruptions happen, nearby suppliers will temporarily support operations.

REQUEST 4:
Activity 1 – Project Schedule Preparation:
For project scheduling activities, the Project Manager is responsible and accountable for preparing timelines and coordinating implementation plans. The Interior Design Team is consulted regarding construction timing, while the HR Team and Marketing Team are informed about the project schedule.
Activity 2 – Market and Location Research:
For market analysis and site selection, the Project Manager is responsible for evaluating and approving final store locations. The Marketing Team is consulted regarding customer potential and market demand, while the Interior Design Team provides consultation about layout suitability. The HR Team is informed about selected locations.
Activity 3 – Store Design and Renovation:
For store design and renovation, the Interior Design Team is responsible for developing store concepts and supervising decoration work. The Project Manager is accountable for ensuring that construction activities meet deadlines and quality standards. Other teams are informed about project progress.
Activity 4 – Staff Recruitment:
For recruitment activities, the HR Team is responsible and accountable for hiring employees and organizing orientation programs. The Project Manager is consulted regarding workforce planning, while the Marketing Team is consulted for customer service training support.
Activity 5 – Advertising and Promotion:
For promotional activities, the Marketing Team is responsible for planning advertisements, social media campaigns, and grand opening events. The Project Manager is accountable for approving campaign strategies. The Interior Design Team and HR Team are informed about campaign schedules and branding activities.
Activity 6 – Supplier Coordination:
For supplier management, the Project Manager is responsible and accountable for selecting suppliers and negotiating contracts. The Interior Design Team is consulted regarding equipment quality and technical requirements, while the remaining teams are informed about supply arrangements.
