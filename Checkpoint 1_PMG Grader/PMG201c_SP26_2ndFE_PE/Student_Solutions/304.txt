Request 1: Narrative Charter Statement
1. Project Name
Coi Cafe Central Expansion Project 2026
2. Justification (Purpose & Reasons)
The purpose of this project is to expand the Coi Cafe into five Central provinces including Da Nang, Hue, Quang Nam, Quang Ngai, and Binh Dinh by establishing one flagship store in each location within 12 months.
This project is implemented to:
Strengthen brand presence nationwide beyond Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City 
Capture growing demand for traditional Vietnamese coffee in Central Vietnam 
Promote sustainable business practices (reusable cups, direct sourcing from farmers) 
Increase revenue and long-term profitability 
3. Project Constraints
Scope Constraint
Open 5 flagship stores (1 per province) 
Includes: site selection, interior design, construction, HR recruitment & training, supply chain setup, marketing campaigns 
Must follow Coi Cafe brand identity & sustainability standards 
Time Constraint
Total duration: 12 months 
Each store must reach break-even within 8 months after opening 
Cost/Budget Constraint
Total budget: VND 8.5 billion 
Approx. VND 1.7 billion per store 
Funding sources: equity + bank loans 
Quality Constraint
Maintain consistent nostalgic brand design & customer experience 
Ensure high-quality coffee sourcing (direct from farmers) 
Achieve customer satisfaction & brand consistency across all locations.
Request 2: Cost/Budget Items
1. Site Acquisition & Rental
Description: Cost for renting or leasing store locations in each province 
Estimation: ~ VND 400 million/store 
Method: Market survey + benchmarking nearby commercial locations 
Responsible: Market Development Director + Finance Team 
2. Interior Design & Construction
Description: Design and build nostalgic themed interiors 
Estimation: ~ VND 600 million/store 
Method: Quotation from contractors + previous store cost data 
Responsible: Interior Design Team 
3. Equipment & Furniture
Description: Coffee machines, furniture, reusable cups system 
Estimation: ~ VND 300 million/store 
Method: Supplier quotations + historical procurement data 
Responsible: Procurement Team 
4. Recruitment & Training
Description: Hiring and training local staff 
Estimation: ~ VND 150 million/store 
Method: HR cost estimation (salary, training sessions, trainers) 
Responsible: HR Team 
5. Marketing & Launch Campaign
Description: Promotional campaigns for each store opening 
Estimation: ~ VND 250 million/store 
Method: Marketing budget planning + past campaign analysis 
Responsible: Marketing Team

Request 3: Project Risks
1. Risk: Delay in Construction
Description: Construction may take longer than planned 
Impact: 
Time: Delay store opening 
Cost: Increased labor/material costs 
Scope/Quality: Rushed work reduces quality 
Mitigation: 
Select experienced contractors 
Set strict milestones 
Contingency: 
Add buffer time 
Allocate backup contractors 
2. Risk: Supply Chain Disruption
Description: Difficulty sourcing coffee or materials from Central suppliers 
Impact: 
Cost: Higher procurement cost 
Quality: Inconsistent product quality 
Mitigation: 
Build multiple supplier relationships 
Sign long-term contracts 
Contingency: 
Use backup suppliers from Dak Lak 
3. Risk: Low Customer Demand
Description: New markets may not respond well 
Impact: 
Cost: Revenue loss 
Time: Longer break-even period 
Scope: May need to adjust concept 
Mitigation: 
Conduct market research 
Adapt menu to local taste 
Contingency: 
Increase marketing budget 
Offer promotions/discounts
