 Request 1:

Project name:
Coi Cafe Central Lean Startup Expansion Project

Justifications:
Expand Coi Cafe into five Central provinces: Da Nang, Hue, Quang Nam, Quang Ngai, and Binh Dinh.
Apply a lean startup approach by testing store assumptions, customer demand, menu preference, and launch messages before full operation.
Reduce unnecessary spending by validating market demand before finalizing store layout, promotion, and operating plan.
Introduce Coi Cafe’s nostalgic Vietnamese coffee space, traditional drink menu, reusable cups, and direct coffee sourcing from Dak Lak farmers to Central Vietnam.
Use customer feedback from early testing and soft activities to improve store readiness.
Build a practical Central expansion model that can be reused for future markets.
Increase revenue from five new stores and support the break-even target within eight months after opening.

Project constraints:
Scope:
Open five flagship stores only, one store in each selected province.
Project scope includes: lean market testing, premises selection, lease agreement, store design, construction, equipment setup, recruitment, training, supplier setup, launch campaign, customer feedback review, and break-even tracking.
The project does not include franchising, extra stores, online-only business, or product lines outside Coi Cafe’s main menu.
Time:
The project duration is 12 months.
All five flagship stores must be opened within the approved timeline.
Market testing and feedback review must not delay the 12-month completion target.
Each store must reach break-even within eight months after opening.
Cost / Budget:
Total budget is VND 8.5 billion.
Average budget is approximately VND 1.7 billion per store.
Funding comes from equity and commercial bank loans.
Budget must be released carefully based on validated needs and approved project stages.
Quality:
All stores must follow Coi Cafe’s core brand standards.
Interior design must remain nostalgic and consistent with the brand.
Drink quality, service, hygiene, and sustainability practices must be controlled.
Feedback-based adjustments must not damage brand consistency or flagship quality.

Request 2:

Line 1: Lean market testing
Name: Customer demand and concept testing
Description: Cost for testing customer interest, preferred locations, drink preferences, price acceptance, and launch messages before major spending decisions.
Estimation: VND 750 million total, around VND 150 million per store.
Way to estimate: Based on survey cost, customer interviews, small testing activities, competitor visits, travel expenses, and feedback analysis.

Name: Location selection and lease arrangement
Description: Cost for site search, traffic observation, legal review, lease negotiation, rental deposit, first rent, and lease documentation.
Estimation: VND 1.45 billion total, around VND 290 million per store.
Way to estimate: Based on local rental prices, required deposit, broker fee, legal review cost, and first-month rent.

Name: Core flagship construction and interior design
Description: Cost for construction, basic nostalgic decoration, furniture, lighting, counter, seating area, signage, and essential brand elements.
Estimation: VND 3.0 billion total, around VND 600 million per store.
Way to estimate: Based on standard design package, contractor quotations, store size, material prices, and required furniture list.

Name: Equipment, staff, and training setup
Description: Cost for coffee machines, grinders, refrigerators, POS system, water filters, reusable cups, kitchen tools, local staff recruitment, uniforms, training, and pre-opening salary.

Way to estimate: Based on equipment checklist, supplier quotations, number of employees, training schedule, trainer cost, uniform price, and salary support.
Person in charge: HR Team, Procurement Team, and Operations Team.

Person in charge: Procurement and Supply Chain Team, and Marketing Team.

Request 3:

Risk 1: Testing results are misleading
Risk name: Early market testing does not reflect real demand.
Description: Survey participants or early testers may show interest, but actual paying customers may behave differently after store opening.
Impacts:
Scope / quality: Store concept or campaign may not match real customer behavior.
Time: More time may be needed to adjust after launch.
Cost: Extra marketing, menu communication, or service correction may occur.
Mitigation plan: Combine surveys with real competitor observation, location traffic checks, and small paid trial activities.
Contingency plan: Adjust campaign messages, product bundles, and customer targeting based on actual sales data after launch.

Risk 2: Lean approach causes underinvestment
Risk name: Cost-saving decisions reduce flagship quality.
Description: Trying to reduce unnecessary spending may lead the team to cut important design, training, or launch activities.
Impacts:
Scope / quality: Store quality may not meet Coi Cafe’s flagship standards.
Time: Rework may be required before opening.
Cost: Correction cost may be higher than initial savings.
Mitigation plan: Define non-negotiable brand and quality standards before applying cost-saving decisions.
Contingency plan: Restore critical quality items using approved reserve and delay only non-essential activities.

Risk 3: Feedback changes create scope creep
Risk name: Too many customer feedback changes.
Description: The team may receive many suggestions during testing and try to adjust store design, menu communication, or launch plan too often.
Impacts:
Scope / quality: The project scope may become unclear.
Time: Revisions may delay construction or launch preparation.
Cost: Additional design, training, and marketing changes may increase cost.
Mitigation plan: Set a clear approval process for feedback-based changes and accept only changes that support break-even and brand standards.
Contingency plan: Freeze design and launch plan after a defined deadline and move other suggestions to post-opening improvement.
