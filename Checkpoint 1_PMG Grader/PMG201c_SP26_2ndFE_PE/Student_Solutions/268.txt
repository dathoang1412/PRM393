REQUEST 1

- Project name

Coi Cafe Central Vietnam Business Expansion Project

The Coi Cafe Central Vietnam Business Expansion Project is created to help the company enter new markets in the Central region. The main purpose of the project is to open five flagship stores in Da Nang, Hue, Quang Nam, Quang Ngai, and Binh Dinh. The project will last 12 months. At the end of the project, all five stores should be ready for daily operation and should follow the same business standards as the current Coi Cafe stores.

The project includes the full process of opening new stores. The first part is to study the market in each province. The company needs to know customer habits, local coffee culture, competitors, rental prices, and suitable business areas. The second part is to choose store locations and complete rental contracts. The third part is to design and renovate the stores. The fourth part is to prepare machines, furniture, raw materials, suppliers, and operating systems. The final part is to recruit employees, train them, promote the stores, and open the stores officially.

The Market Development Director will be the project manager. The project manager will control the project schedule, budget, quality, and communication between teams. The finance team will support budgeting and cost control. The design and construction team will manage store design and renovation. The HR team will recruit and train employees. The marketing team will create campaigns for the new stores. The operations team will prepare equipment, suppliers, inventory, and daily operating procedures.

- Project justification

This project is needed because Coi Cafe wants to grow from a city based coffee chain into a stronger national brand. The company already has experience in Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City. However, if the company only focuses on these two markets, its growth may become limited. Expanding to Central Vietnam can help the company reach new customers and build a wider market presence.

The Central region has many good opportunities for a coffee brand. Da Nang is a modern city with many tourists, young people, and office workers. Hue has strong cultural value and many students. Quang Nam, Quang Ngai, and Binh Dinh are developing areas with increasing demand for modern food and beverage services. These locations can help Coi Cafe test different customer groups and build a stronger regional network.

The project also fits the identity of Coi Cafe. The brand focuses on Vietnamese coffee, traditional taste, nostalgic design, reusable cups, and sustainable coffee sourcing from Dak Lak farmers. These values can create a difference from many other coffee shops. If the company communicates these values well, customers may see Coi Cafe not only as a place to drink coffee but also as a brand that respects Vietnamese culture and responsible business.

This project can also bring financial and operational benefits. The five new stores can create new revenue and improve the companys growth potential. The company can also learn how to manage expansion in different provinces. This knowledge will be useful for future projects in other regions of Vietnam.

- Project constraints

The project has a scope constraint. The project only includes five flagship stores in five selected provinces and cities. The project does not include online only business, franchising, or opening more stores in other regions during the same period. The stores must follow the main Coi Cafe concept and cannot be changed into a different brand style.

The project also has a time constraint. The project must be completed within 12 months. This time limit is challenging because opening a store requires many connected tasks. If the team selects locations late, construction will start late. If construction is delayed, recruitment, training, and marketing may also be affected. Therefore, the schedule must be monitored closely.

- The project has a cost constraint. The approved budget is VND 8.5 billion. This budget must be used for five stores and many different activities. The project team must compare prices, control contracts, and avoid waste. If the project exceeds the budget, the company may have more financial pressure and lower profit in the first period.

- The project also has a quality constraint. The stores must provide consistent product quality, service quality, and store atmosphere. Customers should feel that every new store belongs to the same Coi Cafe brand. Quality cannot be sacrificed only to save time or money because bad customer experience can damage the companys reputation.


REQUEST 2

Main cost and budget items

1. Local market research cost

This cost is used to understand the market before the company invests in each province. It includes customer surveys, competitor checking, traffic observation, and visits to possible commercial areas. The estimate can be made by calculating the number of research days, travel costs, staff costs, and research materials. The Project Manager and Marketing team will be responsible for this cost because they need market information to support location and marketing decisions.

2. Location rental cost

This cost includes deposit, advance rent, contract fee, and possible agency fee. Location is a key success factor because a coffee store needs enough customer traffic. The estimate can be made by collecting price information from different landlords and comparing rental cost by area, store size, and visibility. The Finance team will help the Project Manager decide whether the rental cost is acceptable compared with expected sales.

3. Store renovation cost

This cost includes repairing the space, building the counter, installing lights, improving the floor and ceiling, painting walls, preparing water and electrical systems, and setting up the store for operation. The estimate can be made by asking contractors to inspect the store and provide detailed quotations. The Design and Construction team will be responsible for this cost and must make sure that the store is built according to Coi Cafes standard.

4. Brand decoration cost

This cost includes menu boards, signs, wall design, decorative objects, photos, plants, and other items that create the feeling of Coi Cafe. This is important because the brand wants to give customers a nostalgic and Vietnamese atmosphere. The estimate can be made from the approved design list and supplier quotations. The Design team will work with the Marketing team to make sure decoration supports the brand image.

5. Store equipment cost

This cost includes coffee machines, coffee grinders, refrigerators, blenders, water filters, cash register, POS system, security cameras, speakers, and basic kitchen tools. These items must be ready before staff training and soft opening. The estimate can be made by creating a standard equipment list for one store, checking supplier prices, and multiplying the quantity for five stores. The Operations team and Procurement team will manage this cost.

REQUEST 3

Project risks and response plans

- Risk 1: The company may choose the wrong target area

A store can fail if it is placed in an area with low customer traffic or customers who do not match the brand. For example, a location may have cheap rent but not enough students, office workers, tourists, or local coffee drinkers. If the company selects the wrong target area, the store may have low sales even if the design and service are good.

This risk affects cost because rent, salaries, and utilities still have to be paid every month. It affects time because the store may need a longer period to reach break even. It affects quality because the company may need to change the store strategy quickly after opening.

The mitigation plan is to use clear criteria before choosing any location. The project team should check foot traffic, nearby offices, schools, tourist places, parking, visibility, rental price, and competitor density. The contingency plan is to increase local marketing, develop delivery sales, create promotions for nearby customer groups, and review the location after three months of operation.

- Risk 2: The opening schedule may be delayed

The project has to open five stores within 12 months, so delay is a serious risk. Delay can happen during rental negotiation, design approval, construction, equipment delivery, staff recruitment, or training. If one activity is late, other activities may also be moved later.

This risk affects time directly because store opening dates can be postponed. It affects cost because the company may pay rent before the store can operate. It also affects marketing because opening campaigns may need to be changed or repeated.

The mitigation plan is to create a detailed project timeline with clear deadlines for each task. The Project Manager should hold weekly progress meetings and update the schedule regularly. The contingency plan is to prepare backup suppliers and contractors, start recruitment early, and allow a small buffer time before the official opening date.

Risk 3: The store design may not fit the local market

Coi Cafe has a strong nostalgic design, but each province has different culture and customer expectations. If the store design is too similar in every place, it may not feel connected to local customers. If the design is changed too much, the brand may lose consistency.

This risk affects quality because store atmosphere is part of the customer experience. It can also affect cost if the company needs to redesign or add decoration after opening. It may affect sales if customers do not feel attracted to the store.

The mitigation plan is to keep the main Coi Cafe design style but add small local details in each store. For example, the store can use local cultural images, local materials, or decoration colors that still match the main brand. The contingency plan is to collect customer feedback during soft opening and make small decoration adjustments before the official launch.
