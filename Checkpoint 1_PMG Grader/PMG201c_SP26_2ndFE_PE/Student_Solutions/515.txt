Request 1:

Project name:
Coi Cafe Central Digital-First Expansion Project

Justifications:
Expand Coi Cafe into five Central provinces: Da Nang, Hue, Quang Nam, Quang Ngai, and Binh Dinh.
Use digital channels before and after store opening to build brand awareness faster in new markets.
Promote Coi Cafe’s nostalgic Vietnamese coffee space, traditional drink menu, reusable cup model, and direct sourcing from Dak Lak farmers.
Reduce market entry risk by using online surveys, social media engagement, and pre-launch campaigns to test customer interest.
Attract young customers, students, office workers, tourists, and local communities through digital storytelling.
Build a Central region supply chain to support stable daily operations for all five stores.
Increase revenue outside Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City and support the target that each store reaches break-even within eight months after opening.

Project constraints:
Scope:
Open five flagship stores only, one store in each selected province.
Project scope includes: digital market research, premises selection, lease agreement, store design, construction, equipment setup, local staff recruitment, training, supplier setup, digital pre-launch campaign, grand opening, and performance tracking.
The project does not include franchising, extra provinces, new product lines, or delivery-only business expansion.
Time:
The total project duration is 12 months.
All five stores must be opened within the approved timeline.
Digital pre-launch activities must start before each store opening.
Each store must reach break-even within eight months after opening.
Cost / Budget:
Total budget is VND 8.5 billion.
Average budget is approximately VND 1.7 billion per store.
Funding comes from company equity and commercial bank loans.
Spending must be controlled across premises, construction, equipment, staffing, supply chain, and marketing.
Quality:
All stores must follow Coi Cafe’s brand standards.
Interior design must reflect the nostalgic Vietnamese coffee concept.
Drink quality, service quality, hygiene, and reusable cup practices must be consistent across all stores.
Digital content must communicate the brand accurately and professionally.

Request 3:

Risk 1: Digital campaign creates high expectations that stores cannot meet
Risk name: Gap between online promotion and actual store experience.
Description: Digital content may attract customers strongly, but if the actual store experience is weaker than expected, customers may be disappointed.
Impacts:
Scope / quality: Brand trust and customer experience may be damaged.
Time: Extra time may be needed to improve service, store layout, or product presentation.
Cost: Additional training, service correction, and customer recovery promotions may increase costs.
Mitigation plan: Ensure digital messages match the actual store design, menu, service level, and sustainable practices. Review all campaign content before publishing.
Contingency plan: Respond quickly to customer feedback, improve weak service points, and launch recovery promotions for early customers.

Risk 2: Low online engagement before launch
Risk name: Pre-launch digital activities fail to attract attention.
Description: Customers in some provinces may not engage with Coi Cafe’s online campaigns because the brand is still unfamiliar.
Impacts:
Scope / quality: Store launch may not create strong market awareness.
Time: More time may be needed to build customer traffic after opening.
Cost: Additional advertising and promotion costs may be required.
Mitigation plan: Use province-specific content, local community pages, student groups, and small local influencers before opening.
Contingency plan: Increase offline promotion near the store, use opening vouchers, and cooperate with nearby schools, offices, and communities.

Risk 3: Digital feedback shows weak product-market fit
Risk name: Customer feedback shows limited interest in some provinces.
Description: Online surveys or pre-launch responses may show that certain provinces have lower interest, different taste preferences, or stronger competitors.
Impacts:
Scope / quality: The store concept may need minor adjustment before opening.
Time: Additional research or campaign adjustment may delay launch preparation.
Cost: Extra marketing, menu communication, or local adaptation cost may occur.
Mitigation plan: Test customer preferences early and adjust communication, product bundles, and pricing before store launch.
Contingency plan: Focus promotion on best-matching customer segments and use short-term trial offers to increase adoption.
