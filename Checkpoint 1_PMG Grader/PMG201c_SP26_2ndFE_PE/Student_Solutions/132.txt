Request 1:
Project Title
Central Region Market Entry Project – Coi Café (2026)
Project Purpose and Rationale
Expand the company’s footprint into Central Vietnam, moving beyond its current concentration in major cities.
Take advantage of rising consumer interest in traditional Vietnamese coffee and eco-friendly practices.
Enhance nationwide brand visibility through strategically located flagship outlets.
Build new supplier relationships within the Central region to diversify sourcing.
Strengthen financial performance and reduce reliance on existing markets.
Project Limitations and Conditions
Scope Definition
Launch five flagship cafés in Da Nang, Hue, Quang Nam, Quang Ngai, and Binh Dinh.
Key workstreams include: location scouting, store development, workforce preparation, supply chain establishment, and promotional rollout.
Timeframe
The entire project must be completed within one year (12 months).
Each store is required to achieve financial break-even within 8 months post-launch.
Financial Constraint
The total available budget is VND 8.5 billion, averaging VND 1.7 billion per outlet.
Funding is secured through internal capital and external bank loans.
Quality Expectations
Ensure consistency with Coi Café’s brand identity, including its nostalgic ambiance and traditional menu.
Maintain environmentally responsible operations such as reusable packaging and ethical sourcing.
Deliver a high level of customer service through structured staff training programs.

Request 2:
1. Property Leasing and Fit-out
Details: Includes rental deposits, lease payments, and interior renovation.
Estimated Cost: ~VND 700 million per store.
Estimation Method: Based on previous store data and contractor quotations.
Responsible: Project Manager and Design Team.

2. Equipment and Fixtures
Details: Covers coffee-making equipment and in-store furniture.
Estimated Cost: ~VND 300 million per store.
Estimation Method: Itemized costing supported by supplier quotations.
Responsible: Operations and Procurement teams.

3. Workforce Expenses
Details: Recruitment, wages, and initial training programs.
Estimated Cost: ~VND 200 million per store.
Estimation Method: Calculated using staffing levels and average salary rates.
Responsible: HR department.

4. Promotional Activities
Details: Advertising, branding, and grand opening events.
Estimated Cost: ~VND 150 million per store.
Estimation Method: Derived from past campaigns and projected revenue proportions.
Responsible: Marketing team.

5. Supply Chain Initialization
Details: Establishing supplier contracts, logistics, and initial inventory.
Estimated Cost: ~VND 150 million per store.
Estimation Method: Based on supplier pricing and pilot agreements.
Responsible: Supply Chain Manager.

Request 3:
Risk A: Delays in Store Setup
Description: Construction or licensing delays may slow down store openings.
Potential Effects:
Time: Project schedule overruns
Cost: Increased expenses due to delays
Quality: Reduced quality if deadlines are rushed
Response Strategy:
Mitigation: Careful contractor selection and timeline monitoring
Contingency: Include schedule buffers of 2–3 weeks per location

Risk B: Slow Revenue Growth
Description: Stores may not meet expected revenue targets within the required period.
Potential Effects:
Cost: Lower income and delayed returns
Time: Extended break-even timeline
Response Strategy:
Mitigation: Conduct market research and execute strong pre-opening marketing
Contingency: Adjust pricing strategies and increase promotional efforts

Risk C: Supplier Reliability Issues
Description: Problems with supplier quality or delivery consistency.
Potential Effects:
Quality: Inconsistent product standards
Cost: Additional sourcing costs
Time: Operational disruptions
Response Strategy:
Mitigation: Engage multiple suppliers and enforce quality checks
Contingency: Use backup suppliers from Dak Lak
