REQUEST 1
1. Project Name
Coi Cafe Central Region Expansion Project
2. Justification
Coi Cafe has successfully built a strong brand identity in Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City through its nostalgic design, traditional Vietnamese coffee menu, and sustainable business practices. However, the Central region of Vietnam remains an untapped market with high tourism potential, growing urban populations, and increasing demand for unique café experiences.
This project aims to expand Coi Cafe’s presence into five Central provinces (Da Nang, Hue, Quang Nam, Quang Ngai, and Binh Dinh) by establishing one flagship store in each location. The expansion will strengthen brand recognition nationwide, diversify revenue streams, and reduce dependency on existing markets. In addition, sourcing raw materials from Central suppliers supports sustainability goals and strengthens local partnerships. The project also serves as a strategic step toward scaling the brand across Vietnam.
3. Project Constraints
Scope constraints:
 The project includes site surveying and selection, store design and construction, recruitment and training of local staff, supply chain setup with Central suppliers, and marketing campaigns for each store. It excludes future franchising and international expansion.
Time constraints:
 All five flagship stores must be completed and operational within 12 months.
Cost/Budget constraints:
 The total budget is limited to VND 8.5 billion (approximately VND 1.7 billion per store), funded by equity and bank loans.
Quality constraints:
 Each store must maintain consistent brand identity (nostalgic design, product quality, sustainability practices) and achieve break-even within 8 months after opening.

REQUEST 2
1. Site Rental and Deposit Costs
Description: Costs for renting and securing premises in five provinces.
 Estimation: Based on average rental price per m^2 in each city × store size + 3–6 months deposit.
 Method: Market research and real estate quotations.
 Responsible: Project Manager + Finance Team.
2. Interior Design and Construction
Description: Store design, renovation, furniture, and decoration aligned with Coi’s nostalgic concept.
 Estimation: Estimated per store based on previous projects (VND 700–900 million/store).
 Method: Contractor quotations + historical cost data.
 Responsible: Interior Design Team.
3. Equipment and Tools
Description: Coffee machines, grinders, refrigerators, POS systems, reusable cup systems.
 Estimation: Unit cost × quantity per store.
 Method: Supplier quotations and standard equipment list.
 Responsible: Operations Team.
4. Recruitment and Training Costs
Description: Hiring local staff, onboarding, and training programs.
 Estimation: Number of employees × salary during training + training material costs.
 Method: HR planning and salary benchmarking.
 Responsible: HR Team.
5. Marketing and Launch Campaigns
Description: Opening campaigns, local promotions, social media marketing.
 Estimation: Fixed budget per store (VND 100–150 million/store).
 Method: Marketing budget allocation and past campaign comparison.
 Responsible: Marketing Team.
6. Raw Material Supply Setup 
Description: Building supply chain with Central region suppliers.
 Estimation: Initial procurement + logistics setup costs.
 Method: Supplier contracts and logistics estimation.
 Responsible: Supply Chain Team.

REQUEST 3
Risk 1: Difficulty in Securing Suitable Locations
Description: Prime locations may be unavailable or too expensive.
 Impact:
Scope/Quality: Poor location affects brand positioning
Time: Delays store opening
Cost: Higher rental or relocation cost
Response:
Mitigation: Early site scouting and multiple options
Contingency: Adjust store size or choose secondary locations with strong marketing support
Risk 2: Inconsistent Brand Experience Across Stores
Description: Differences in design, service, or product quality across provinces.
 Impact:
Quality: Damages brand identity
Time: Requires rework and retraining
Cost: Additional training and redesign costs
Response:
Mitigation: Standardized design guidelines and training manuals
Contingency: Conduct regular audits and deploy experienced staff to support new stores
Risk 3: Failure to Reach Break-even in 8 Months
Description: Low customer demand or ineffective operations.
 Impact:
Cost: Financial loss
Time: Extended recovery period
Scope: May delay further expansion
Response:
Mitigation: Strong pre-launch marketing and location analysis
Contingency: Adjust pricing, promotions, and reduce operational costs.
