Request 1: Establish Two Project Objectives Using the SMART Framework
Objective 1:

Open and operate five Cofi Cafe flagship stores in Central Vietnam within the planned 12-month project timeline.

Specific: The project will establish one store in each selected province: Da Nang, Hue, Quang Nam, Quang Ngai, and Binh Dinh.
Measurable: The objective can be measured by the number of stores completed, equipped, staffed, and officially opened.
Achievable: This goal is achievable because Cofi Cafe already has practical experience from operating 12 stores in Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City.
Relevant: The objective is directly connected to Cofi’s growth strategy and helps the brand enter a new regional market.
Time-bound: All five stores must be launched within 12 months.
Objective 2:

Ensure all five new stores are ready to deliver consistent service quality and stable product supply before opening.

Specific: This includes staff training, supplier preparation, store operating procedures, and quality checks before launch.
Measurable: Progress can be tracked through completed training records, approved supplier lists, operation checklists, and pre-opening inspection results.
Achievable: This is realistic because the company can apply its current store standards and adjust them to fit local conditions.
Relevant: Consistent service and supply are important for protecting Cofi Cafe’s brand reputation in new provinces.
Time-bound: These preparations must be completed before each store’s official opening date.

Request 3: Define at least 5 Quality Assurance (QA) or Quality Control (QC) Activities for the Project
1. Market and Site Review (QA):

Conducted in the early planning stage to make sure each selected province and location has enough business potential. The team reviews customer traffic, rental cost, competition, visibility, and accessibility. This helps reduce the chance of choosing a weak store location.

2. Cost Control Review (QA):

Performed throughout the project to ensure spending stays within the approved VND 8.5 billion budget. The project manager and finance team compare planned and actual costs for rent, construction, equipment, training, and marketing. This helps prevent budget overrun.

3. Supplier Screening (QA):

Conducted before confirming suppliers for coffee beans and other materials. The team checks product quality, delivery reliability, price stability, and supplier capacity. This helps ensure the new stores can maintain stable product quality after opening.

4. Interior Design Verification (QA):

Performed before construction begins. The design team and management review the layout, lighting, furniture, decoration, and customer space. This ensures each store still reflects Cofi Cafe’s traditional and nostalgic atmosphere.

5. Construction and Equipment Inspection (QC):

Performed during and after construction. The team checks construction quality, electrical systems, furniture placement, equipment installation, and safety conditions. This helps identify problems before the store opens.

6. Staff Performance Test (QC):

Conducted after staff training. Employees are tested through practical tasks such as preparing drinks, serving customers, using the POS system, and handling basic customer issues. This ensures the team is ready for real operations.

7. Trial Opening Review (QC):

Conducted before the grand opening. Each store runs a limited trial operation to test service flow, drink quality, staff coordination, equipment function, and customer response. The team can then fix problems before the official launch.
