Request 1:  Narrative charter statement

Project name:
Coi Cafe Central Vietnam Expansion Project

Justifications:

Expand Coi Cafe’s market from Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City to 5 Central provinces: Da Nang, Hue, Quang Nam, Quang Ngai, and Binh Dinh.
Increase brand awareness and customer reach in Central Vietnam.
Take advantage of growing demand for Vietnamese coffee, nostalgic coffee spaces, and sustainable business models.
Strengthen Coi Cafe’s brand image through reusable cups, traditional drinks, and direct coffee purchasing from Dak Lak farmers.
Build a new regional supply chain to support long-term operations in Central Vietnam.
Increase revenue through 5 new flagship stores.
Achieve financial growth by requiring each store to reach break-even within 8 months after opening.

Project constraints:

Scope:

Open 5 flagship stores only, one in each selected province.
Project scope includes:
Premises survey and selection
Interior design and construction
Local staff recruitment and training
Raw material supply chain setup
Store launch marketing campaigns
No additional provinces or store formats are added during this project.

Time:

Total project duration: 12 months.
All 5 stores must be opened within the 12-month schedule.
Each store must reach break-even within 8 months after opening.

Cost / Budget:

Total budget: VND 8.5 billion.
Average budget per store: about VND 1.7 billion.
Funding sources: equity and commercial bank loans.
Cost must be controlled to avoid exceeding the approved budget.

Quality:

All stwores must follow Coi Cafe’s brand standards.
Store design must reflect the nostalgic Vietnamese coffee concept.
Drink qualityw, customer service, and sustainable practices must be consistent across all stores.
Construction and equipment must be suitable for flagship store operations.

Request 3: 

Risk 1: Delay in premises selection

Risk name: Unsuitable or delayed store location selection
Description: Good locations may be difficult to find because of high rent, legal issues, or long lease negotiation.
Impacts:
Scope / quality: May force the team to choose a weaker location.
Time: Delays construction and store opening.
Cost: Higher rental deposit, extra survey cost, or increased marketing cost.
Mitigation plan: Prepare clear site selection criteria and shortlist at least two backup locations in each province.
Contingency plan: Adjust the opening order and launch first in provinces with confirmed premises.

Risk 2: Construction cost overrun

Risk name: Interior construction exceeds budget
Description: Material prices, contractor fees, or design changes may increase construction costs.
Impacts:
Scope / quality: Some design elements may be reduced or changed.
Time: Rework or contractor delay may postpone opening.
Cost: Total project spending may exceed VND 8.5 billion.
Mitigation plan: Use standard design guidelines, fixed contractor quotations, and weekly construction progress checks.
Contingency plan: Use contingency reserve and reduce non-essential decoration while keeping core brand identity.

Risk 3: Store fails to reach break-even within 8 months

Risk name: Lower-than-expected sales performance
Description: New stores may attract fewer customers due to weak local awareness, strong competitors, or ineffective marketing.
Impacts:
Scope / quality: Pressure to reduce service quality or cut operating activities.
Time: Break-even period becomes longer than 8 months.
Cost: Higher operating losses and additional marketing expenses.
Mitigation plan: Conduct market research, design local campaigns, train staff carefully, and monitor sales weekly.
Contingency plan: Launch extra promotions, cooperate with local communities, adjust menu/pricing, and increase marketing for weak stores.
