Request 1:
Project name:
Coi Cafe Central Feedback-Driven Expansion Project
Justifications:
Coi Cafe expands into Da Nang, Hue, Quang Nam, Quang Ngai, and Binh Dinh with one flagship store in each province.
The project uses customer feedback as the main management tool to improve store design, launch campaigns, service quality, and post-opening operations.
This helps Coi Cafe enter new markets more carefully instead of assuming all Central provinces have the same customer needs.
The project still maintains Coi Cafe’s core strengths: nostalgic Vietnamese space, traditional drink menu, reusable cups, and direct sourcing from Dak Lak farmers.
Customer feedback will help reduce market risk and improve local acceptance.
A Central region supply chain and trained local staff will be developed to support stable operation.
The project aims to create new revenue and ensure each store reaches break-even within eight months after opening.
Project constraints:
Scope:
The project opens only five flagship stores, one in each selected province.
The project scope includes: customer research, premises selection, lease agreement, store design, construction, equipment setup, staff recruitment, training, supplier setup, feedback collection, launch campaign, and break-even tracking.
The project does not include franchising, extra stores, unrelated digital platforms, or major new product development.
Time:
Project duration is 12 months.
All five stores must be opened within the approved timeline.
Feedback collection must support decision-making but cannot delay the full project timeline.
Each store must reach break-even within eight months after opening.
Cost / Budget:
Total budget is VND 8.5 billion.
Average budget is around VND 1.7 billion per store.
Funding comes from equity and commercial bank loans.
Spending must be controlled across all major activities.
Quality:
All stores must follow Coi Cafe’s brand standards.
Drink quality, service, hygiene, sustainable practices, and interior design must remain consistent.
Customer feedback may guide minor improvements but cannot change the core brand identity.

Request 3:
Risk 1:
Risk name:edback is collected but not used effectively.
Description: The team may collect customer feedback but fail to analyze or apply it to improve operations.
Possible impacts:
Scope / quality: Customer problems may continue after launch.
Time: More time may be needed to correct issues later.
Cost: Extra service recovery and marketing correction costs may occur.
Mitigation plan: Assign clear responsibility for feedback review and hold weekly feedback meetings during the first month.
Contingency plan: Prioritize the top three repeated customer issues and solve them immediately.
Risk 2:
Risk name: Too much feedback causes unclear decisions.
Description: Different customers may give conflicting suggestions about design, menu, price, or service.
Possible impacts:
Scope / quality: The project may lose focus and weaken brand consistency.
Time: Decision-making may become slower.
Cost: Unnecessary changes may increase spending.
Mitigation plan: Accept only feedback that matches Coi Cafe’s brand, budget, and break-even goals.
Contingency plan: Freeze major decisions after approval and move minor suggestions to post-opening improvement.
Risk 3:
Risk name: Negative feedback affects brand image after opening.
Description: Early customer complaints may spread on social media if service, drink quality, or waiting time is not managed well.
Possible impacts:
Scope / quality: Brand reputation in new provinces may decline.
Time: More time may be needed to rebuild trust.
Cost: Extra promotion, customer recovery, and retraining costs may increase.
Mitigation plan: Monitor customer comments daiy respond quickly to valid complaints.
Contingency plan: Provide service recovery vouchers, fix root causes, and assign store supervisors to monitor service closely.
