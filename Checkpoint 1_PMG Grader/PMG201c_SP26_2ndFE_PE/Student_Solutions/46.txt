Request 1:

Project name:
Coi Cafe Central Loyalty-Based Expansion Project

Justifications:
Expand Coi Cafe into five Central provinces: Da Nang, Hue, Quang Nam, Quang Ngai, and Binh Dinh.
Use customer loyalty as the main strategy to help new stores build repeat visits after opening.
Introduce Coi Cafe’s nostalgic Vietnamese coffee space, traditional drink menu, reusable cup practice, and sustainable sourcing from Dak Lak farmers to Central Vietnam.
Create a stable customer base through membership offers, repeat-visit promotions, student and office customer programs, and local community engagement.
Reduce the risk of high opening traffic but low long-term sales.
Build a Central region supplier and operating system to support five flagship stores.
Increase revenue outside Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City and support the target that each store reaches break-even within eight months after opening.

Project constraints:
Scope:
Open five flagship stores only, one store in each selected province.
Project scope includes: market survey, customer loyalty research, premises selection, lease agreement, store design, construction, equipment setup, recruitment, training, supplier setup, launch campaign, loyalty program activation, and break-even monitoring.
The project does not include franchising, extra stores, unrelated product development, or expansion outside the five assigned provinces.
Time:
The project duration is 12 months.
All five stores must be opened within the approved timeline.
Loyalty activities must be prepared before each store launch.
Each store must reach break-even within eight months after opening.
Cost / Budget:
Total budget is VND 8.5 billion.
Average budget is approximately VND 1.7 billion per store.
Funding comes from company equity and commercial bank loans.
Budget must be controlled across rent, construction, equipment, staff preparation, supply chain, marketing, and loyalty activities.
Quality:
All stores must follow Coi Cafe’s brand standards.
Store design must reflect the nostalgic Vietnamese coffee concept.
Drink quality, service quality, hygiene, and reusable cup practice must be consistent.
Loyalty promotions must support customer retention without damaging the brand image or profit margin.

Request 3:

Risk 1: Loyalty program reduces profit margin
Risk name: Promotions are too costly.
Description: Membership rewards, vouchers, or discounts may attract customers but reduce profit if they are not controlled.
Impacts:
Scope / quality: The store may focus too much on discounts instead of brand value.
Time: More time may be needed to reach break-even.
Cost: Promotion expenses and lower margin may affect profitability.
Mitigation plan: Set clear limits for discounts, focus on repeat-visit rewards, and use bundles instead of deep price cuts.
Contingency plan: Reduce low-margin promotions and shift to non-discount benefits such as priority offers, limited drinks, or reusable cup rewards.

Risk 2: Customers visit only during promotion periods
Risk name: Low retention after promotions end.
Description: Customers may come for opening vouchers but not return once promotions stop.
Impacts:
Scope / quality: The store may fail to build a stable local customer base.
Time: Customer loyalty may take longer to develop.
Cost: Additional marketing and retention activities may be required.
Mitigation plan: Combine opening promotions with product quality, good service, membership collection, and regular customer engagement.
Contingency plan: Launch a monthly loyalty calendar, collect customer feedback, and create targeted offers for repeat customers.

Risk 3: Staff do not explain loyalty activities properly
Risk name: Weak loyalty program execution.
Description: New staff may not explain membership benefits, reusable cup rewards, or repeat-visit offers clearly to customers.
Impacts:
Scope / quality: Customer experience and promotion effectiveness may decrease.
Time: Additional staff training may be needed.
Cost: Wasted marketing budget and extra training cost may occur.
Mitigation plan: Prepare simple loyalty scripts and train staff before launch.
Contingency plan: Use clear in-store posters, QR instructions, and assign store supervisors to coach staff during the first month.
