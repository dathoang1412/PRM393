Request 1:
Project name:
Coi Cafe Central Local Signature Store Project

Justifications:
Expand Coi Cafe into five Central provinces: Da Nang, Hue, Quang Nam, Quang Ngai, and Binh Dinh.
Build each flagship store as a local signature store that keeps Coi Cafe’s core brand but reflects the atmosphere of its province.
Introduce Coi Cafe’s nostalgic Vietnamese coffee space, traditional drink menu, reusable cup practice, and sustainable coffee sourcing to new customers.
Create stronger customer connection by making each store feel familiar to local residents while still belonging to the Coi Cafe brand.
Increase brand awareness outside Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City.
Build a Central region supply chain to support stable store operations.
Generate new revenue and ensure each store reaches break-even within eight months after opening.

Project constraints:
Scope:
Open five flagship stores only, one store in each selected province.
Project scope includes: market survey, premises selection, lease agreement, local signature design concept, construction, equipment setup, recruitment, training, supplier setup, launch campaign, and performance tracking.
Each store may include small local design elements, but the main Coi Cafe brand identity must remain consistent.
The project does not include franchising, extra stores, new product lines, or provinces outside the given five.
Time:
The project duration is 12 months.
All five stores must be opened within the approved timeline.
Each store must reach break-even within eight months after opening.
Cost / Budget:
Total budget is VND 8.5 billion.
Average budget is around VND 1.7 billion per store.
Funding comes from company equity and commercial bank loans.
All costs must be controlled to avoid budget overrun.
Quality:
All stores must follow Coi Cafe’s core brand standards.
Interior design must be nostalgic, consistent, and suitable for flagship store quality.
Drink quality, customer service, hygiene, and sustainable practices must be consistent across all stores.
Local design elements must not conflict with Coi Cafe’s Vietnamese coffee identity.

Request 2:

Budget Group 1: Local insight and concept research
Name: Local insight and store concept research
Description: Cost for studying customer behavior, competitor stores, local culture, suitable store areas, and design ideas that can support a local signature concept.
Estimation: VND 700 million total, around VND 140 million per store.
Way to estimate: Based on field visits, customer interviews, competitor observation, local cultural research, travel cost, and concept testing.
Person in charge: Project Manager and Marketing Team.

Budget Group 2: Premises acquisition
Name: Store premises acquisition and lease setup
Description: Cost for location search, traffic checking, lease negotiation, legal review, rental deposit, and first rent payment.
Estimation: VND 1.5 billion total, around VND 300 million per store.
Way to estimate: Based on local rental rates, deposit requirement, broker fee, legal checking fee, and initial rent.
Person in charge: Project Manager and Finance Team.

Budget Group 3: Local signature interior build
Name: Store construction and local signature interior
Description: Cost for construction, furniture, lighting, counter, seating area, signage, nostalgic decoration, and limited local design details for each province.
Estimation: VND 3.1 billion total, around VND 620 million per store.
Way to estimate: Based on store size, design drawings, contractor quotations, material prices, furniture list, and approved local decoration items.
Person in charge: Interior Design and Construction Team.

Budget Group 4: Operating readiness
Name: Equipment and staff preparation
Description: Cost for coffee machines, grinders, refrigerators, POS system, water filters, reusable cups, kitchen tools, recruitment, training, uniforms, and pre-opening salary.
Estimation: VND 2.2 billion total, around VND 440 million per store.
Way to estimate: Based on standard equipment checklist, supplier quotations, number of staff, training period, trainer cost, uniform cost, and salary support.
Person in charge: Procurement Team, HR Team, and Operations Team.

Budget Group 5: Supply chain and opening promotion
Name: Opening inventory and local signature launch campaign
Description: Cost for supplier selection, first inventory, delivery setup, safety stock, quality checking, grand opening, local promotion, vouchers, and social media content.
Estimation: VND 1.0 billion total, around VND 200 million per store.
Way to estimate: Based on first-month inventory needs, supplier prices, transportation cost, event scale, advertising price, and promotion budget.
Person in charge: Procurement and Supply Chain Team, and Marketing Team.

Request 3:

Risk 1: Local design elements weaken brand consistency
Risk name: Local signature concept conflicts with Coi Cafe identity.
Description: If each store uses too many local elements, the stores may become inconsistent and customers may not clearly recognize Coi Cafe’s brand.
Impacts:
Scope / quality: Brand identity and flagship quality may become inconsistent.
Time: Extra design review or redesign may delay construction.
Cost: Redesign, decoration replacement, or rework may increase cost.
Mitigation plan: Prepare a brand guideline that defines fixed Coi Cafe elements and limited local elements allowed for each store.
Contingency plan: Remove or adjust local elements that conflict with the brand before grand opening.

Risk 2: Local concept does not attract customers
Risk name: Weak customer response to local signature positioning.
Description: Customers may not value the local signature concept if store location, design, or launch message is not relevant to their preferences.
Impacts:
Scope / quality: The store may fail to become a strong flagship location.
Time: More time may be needed to build customer traffic.
Cost: Extra marketing and promotion costs may be needed.
Mitigation plan: Test local concept ideas through customer interviews and competitor analysis before finalizing design and campaign.
Contingency plan: Adjust launch message, add customer promotions, and emphasize Coi Cafe’s core Vietnamese coffee experience.

Risk 3: Supplier and staff quality varies by province
Risk name: Uneven operating quality across provinces.
Description: Different local staff and suppliers may cause differences in service quality, drink taste, or inventory stability.
Impacts:
Scope / quality: Customer experience may not be consistent across the five stores.
Time: Additional training and supplier correction may be required.
Cost: Retraining, supervision, or emergency purchasing may increase costs.
Mitigation plan: Use the same training manual, product recipes, supplier quality checklist, and pre-opening operation test for all stores.
Contingency plan: Send experienced supervisors from existing stores and use backup suppliers until local teams become stable.
