Request 1: Project Charter Document
1. Project Title

Coi Cafe Central Vietnam Market Expansion 2026

2. Project Rationale and Objectives
Expand Coi Cafe is operational footprint into Central Vietnam to achieve wider national coverage.
Leverage growing consumer interest in traditional Vietnamese coffee culture and sustainable consumption practices.
Enhance brand visibility through the development of flagship stores in strategically selected provinces.
Strengthen and diversify the supply chain by engaging with Central region suppliers.
Support long-term revenue growth and reduce dependence on existing metropolitan markets.
3. Project Constraints

Scope

Establish five flagship outlets in Da Nang, Hue, Quang Nam, Quang Ngai, and Binh Dinh.
Project scope includes site acquisition, store design and construction, recruitment and training, supply chain development, and marketing implementation.

Timeframe

The project must be completed within a 12-month period.
Each store is expected to achieve break-even within 8 months after launch.

Budget

Total allocated budget is VND 8.5 billion, equivalent to approximately VND 1.7 billion per store.
Funding sources include internal equity and commercial bank financing.

Quality Requirements

Maintain consistent brand identity featuring nostalgic design and traditional beverage offerings.
Ensure sustainability through eco-friendly materials and responsible sourcing practices.
Deliver consistent service quality through structured staff training programs.
Request 2: Cost Structure Overview
1. Property Leasing and Store Development
Description: Covers lease deposits, rental payments, interior construction, and decoration works.
Estimated Cost: Approximately VND 700 million per store.
Estimation Method: Based on benchmarking from existing outlets and contractor quotations.
Responsible Party: Project Manager and Interior Design Team
2. Equipment and Store Furnishings
Description: Includes coffee machines, brewing tools, refrigeration units, tables, and seating.
Estimated Cost: Approximately VND 300 million per store.
Estimation Method: Bottom-up estimation derived from supplier pricing lists.
Responsible Party: Operations and Procurement Teams
3. Human Resource Costs
Description: Recruitment, salaries, onboarding, and initial training programs.
Estimated Cost: Approximately VND 200 million per store.
Estimation Method: Parametric estimation based on workforce size and salary benchmarks.
Responsible Party: Human Resources Department
4. Marketing and Launch Activities
Description: Advertising campaigns, brand promotion, and store opening events.
Estimated Cost: Approximately VND 150 million per store.
Estimation Method: Derived from historical campaign data and projected revenue ratios.
Responsible Party: Marketing Department
5. Supply Chain Establishment
Description: Supplier onboarding, logistics setup, and initial inventory procurement.
Estimated Cost: Approximately VND 150 million per store.
Estimation Method: Based on supplier quotations and pilot procurement agreements.
Responsible Party: Supply Chain Management Team
Request 3: Risk Identification and Mitigation Plan
Risk 1: Construction Schedule Overruns
Description: Delays may arise due to permitting procedures, contractor performance issues, or material shortages.
Impacts:
Time: Extension of project timeline
Cost: Increased operational and rental expenses
Quality: Reduced construction quality under time pressure
Response Strategy:
Mitigation: Engage reliable contractors and enforce strict timeline tracking
Contingency: Include a buffer of 2–3 weeks per store
Risk 2: Insufficient Revenue Performance
Description: Stores may fail to generate expected revenue within the planned timeframe.
Impacts:
Cost: Reduced profitability and cash flow challenges
Time: Extended break-even period
Response Strategy:
Mitigation: Conduct thorough market research and implement strong pre-launch marketing strategies
Contingency: Adjust pricing models and increase promotional activities
Risk 3: Supply Chain Instability
Description: Disruptions or inconsistencies in supply quality and delivery from regional partners.
Impacts:
Quality: Variability in product standards
Cost: Increased procurement expenses
Time: Operational disruptions
Response Strategy:
Mitigation: Develop multi-supplier arrangements and enforce strict quality control systems
Contingency: Maintain backup suppliers from Dak Lak region
