Request 1:

Project name:
Coi Cafe Central Farm-to-Cup Expansion Project

Justifications:
Expand Coi Cafe into five Central provinces: Da Nang, Hue, Quang Nam, Quang Ngai, and Binh Dinh.
Use the “farm-to-cup” story as the main expansion concept by connecting Dak Lak coffee farmers, Vietnamese coffee culture, and customers in Central Vietnam.
Strengthen Coi Cafe’s sustainable brand image through direct coffee purchasing, reusable cups, and responsible store operations.
Differentiate Coi Cafe from other coffee chains by showing the origin, quality, and cultural value of Vietnamese coffee.
Open one flagship store in each province to increase customer reach and brand visibility.
Build a Central region supply network that supports stable store operations.
Create new revenue outside Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City and help each store reach break-even within eight months after opening.

Project constraints:
Scope:
Open five flagship stores only, one store in each selected province.
Project scope includes: market survey, premises selection, lease agreement, farm-to-cup interior concept, construction, equipment setup, recruitment, training, supplier setup, launch campaign, and break-even tracking.
The project does not include franchising, extra stores, new product lines, or expansion outside the five assigned provinces.
Time:
The total project duration is 12 months.
All five flagship stores must be opened within the approved timeline.
Each store must reach break-even within eight months after opening.
Cost / Budget:
Total budget is VND 8.5 billion.
Average budget is approximately VND 1.7 billion per store.
Funding comes from company equity and commercial bank loans.
Budget must be controlled across rental, construction, equipment, staffing, supply chain, and marketing.
Quality:
All stores must follow Coi Cafe’s brand standards.
Interior design must reflect the nostalgic Vietnamese coffee concept and the farm-to-cup story.
Drink quality, service quality, hygiene, and reusable cup practice must be consistent.
Coffee and raw materials must meet Coi Cafe’s quality standards before store opening.

Request 2:

Budget Category 1: Market and farm-to-cup concept research
Name: Market and farm-to-cup concept research
Description: Cost for studying customer demand, competitor stores, suitable locations, customer interest in Vietnamese coffee origin stories, and local market potential.
Estimation: VND 700 million total, around VND 140 million per store.
Way to estimate: Based on field survey cost, customer interviews, competitor visits, travel expenses, and concept testing.

Name: Store premises selection and lease setup
Description: Cost for site search, traffic checking, legal review, lease negotiation, rental deposit, first rent payment, and basic premises preparation.
Estimation: VND 1.5 billion total, around VND 300 million per store.
Way to estimate: Based on rental prices, deposit requirements, broker fees, legal checking cost, and first rental payment in each province.

Name: Farm-to-cup interior and construction package
Description: Cost for construction, furniture, lighting, service counter, seating area, signage, nostalgic decoration, and design elements showing Vietnamese coffee origin and sustainability.

Way to estimate: Based on store area, contractor quotations, design drawings, material price, furniture list, and approved brand storytelling elements.

Budget Category 4: Equipment and workforce preparation
Name: Equipment and staff readiness
Description: Cost for coffee machines, grinders, refrigerators, POS system, water filters, reusable cups, kitchen tools, staff recruitment, uniforms, training, and pre-opening salary support.

Way to estimate: Based on standard equipment checklist, supplier quotations, number of staff, training duration, trainer cost, uniform cost, and salary support.
Person in charge: Procurement Team, HR Team, and Operations Team.

Budget Category 5: Supply chain and brand story launch

Request 3:

Risk 1: Farm-to-cup story is not clearly understood
Risk name: Weak customer understanding of the brand story.
Description: Customers may not understand or care about the direct coffee sourcing story if the message is too technical or poorly communicated.
Impacts:
Scope / quality: The flagship stores may fail to create a strong brand differentiation.
Time: More time may be needed to revise communication and store storytelling.
Cost: Extra marketing content and customer education cost may occur.
Mitigation plan: Use simple messages, visual storytelling inside the store, staff explanation, and social media content before launch.
Contingency plan: Simplify campaign messages and focus on clear benefits such as Vietnamese coffee quality, farmer support, and sustainable cups.

Risk 2: Coffee quality inconsistency
Risk name: Coffee taste does not remain consistent across stores.
Description: Different staff skills, equipment setup, or storage conditions may affect drink taste and product quality.
Impacts:
Scope / quality: Drink quality may not match Coi Cafe standards.
Time: Additional testing and retraining may be required.
Cost: Extra training, quality inspection, and product replacement cost may increase.
Mitigation plan: Use standard recipes, equipment calibration, staff drink tests, and quality control before opening.
Contingency plan: Send experienced baristas from current stores to retrain local staff and inspect drink quality after launch.

Risk 3: Supply and storytelling materials are delayed
Risk name: Launch materials and supply setup are not ready on time.
Description: Opening inventory, reusable cups, signage, storytelling visuals, or supplier documents may arrive late.
Impacts:
Scope / quality: Store launch may look incomplete or inconsistent with the farm-to-cup concept.
Time: Soft opening or grand opening may be delayed.
Cost: Urgent delivery or replacement materials may increase cost.
Mitigation plan: Prepare procurement schedule early, confirm suppliers in advance, and track delivery status weekly.
Contingency plan: Launch with essential materials first and add non-critical storytelling elements after opening without affecting core operations.
