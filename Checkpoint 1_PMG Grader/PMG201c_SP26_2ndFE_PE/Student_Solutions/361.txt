REQUEST 1 :
Project Name:
- Coi Cafe Expansion Project
Justifications :
- To further extend the reach of Coi Cafe to more customers across Da Nang,Hue Quang Nam, Quang Ngai and Binh Dinh provinces of Vietnam.
- Reasons to Implement:
- There is a grow demand for sustainable as well as locally sourced products.
- Due to an overcrow retailer environment in Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh city.
- We will seek to build new customer bases and brand awareness in the central provinces.
- The operational model of our business is based on the use of reusable cups, as well as the direct purchase of goods from local farmers and suppliers.

Project Constraints :
- Scope:
- Survey and select new new store location.
- Design and construct the store interiors.
- Recruit and train local personnel.
- Development of new supply chains for procurement of raw and roasted coffee materials.
- Launch marketing campaigns for each new store.
- Time:
- Project Completion: 12 months. Between each retail store opening: 2 months.
- Break-even in 8 months.
- Cost/Budget:
- Total budget for the project are VND 8.5 billion. The budget for each store are VND 1.7 billion , which will be funded by equity and commercial bank loans.
- Quality:
- The concept stores must comply with the brand’s design, offer high quality coffee and consistency across all locations.

REQUEST 3 :
List 3 project risks including risk title/name, description of the risk, possible positive and negative impacts of the risk on the project and the corresponding project risk management plan, i.e. risk response plans - mitigation plans and contingency plans.

1. Risk: Delay in Store Opening
- Opening of Stores subject to delay due to Building or Supply chain related reasons.
- Potential Consequences: Reduced Revenue, Delay of Customer Interactions, Missed Break-Even Point.
- Mitigation: Incorporate delay time into plan, update for weekly progress review, and engage in early dialogue with supplier.
- Contingency: Be prepared to postpone the date of opening the store and to inform the customers of any changes.

2. Risk: Supply Chain Disruptions
- Sourcing : Delays associated with the sourcing of coffee beans as well as local construction materials.
- Uncertainty: Inconsistent quality of delivered products, and potentially serious effects on normal business operations.
- Mitigation: Sourcing from multiple vendors and/or developing long-term agreements can help mitigate this risk.
- Keep watch of contingency: Respond rapidly to any disruptions by finding alternative suppliers and keeping customers informed of any shortages.

3. Risk: Low Consumer Demand in New Locations
- This new format of stores may not attract sufficient customers in certain locations.
- Slow sales. - Bad reviews. - Low revenue.
- Mitigation: More in-depth market research and local marketing efforts.
- Contingency Level 1 (LLI - Low Low Intensity): Introduce marketing adjustments, loyalty programs and promotion initiatives in order to draw customers.
