Request 1

Narrative Chart

Project Name:

Coi Cafe Expansion Project

Justifications (Purpose & Reasons to Implement the Project):

- Coi Cafe will solidify its position in the Vietnamese market by opening 5 more locations in strategic cities throughout the country.
- We will be serving brand signature beverages, using eco friendly sourcing practices and recreating the same comfortable dining atmosphere as our other locations.
- Increase brand awareness and create loyal customers in order to support long term sustainable growth and expansion of the business geographically.
- Premium Vietnamese coffee products; Sustainable farming practices encouraged.

Project Constraints (In Terms of Scope, Time, Cost/Budget, and Quality):

- Scope: This project scope does not include expansion to additional cities, and this project will only be implemented to 5 cities of Da Nang, Hue, Quang Nam, Quang Ngai, Binh Dinh.
- Timing: All new stores will need to open 12 months from the project start date.
- Budget: VND 85 billion in total; VND 17 billion spent per store for setting up.
- Quality: Interpret and deliver the brand design identity in retail spaces while prioritizing product functionality, environmental responsibility, and engagement.

Request 2

Cost/Budget Estimation

Cost/Budget Details:

- Total estimated budget for project is VND 85 billion (Construction, employees training, purchasing and fit out for 5 outlets)
- Cost : about VND 17 billion for 5 stores

Key Expenses:

- Land use permit for store construction and interior design: VND 800,000,000 per store.
- Recruitment and training of staff: VND 200 million (USD 8,500) per outlet.
- Materials and supply sourcing for Store (Central Kitchen and Delivery Office): VND 500 million per store

Total Estimated Cost for the Entire Project:

- Total estimated investment: VND 85 billion, of which a contingency reserve of VND 15 billion is allowed for possible unexpected costs.

Estimation Method:

- Prices are calculated with reference to local construction costs and rates currently prevailing, as well as quotations from suppliers who have provided materials for previous projects. These need to be treated as a rough guide to assist with budgeting, rather than a definitive quote.
- Estimated future expenditure including potential for inflation, project delays and other unforeseen costs

Request 3

Identify Project Roles

Project Roles:

- Project Manager (PM): Manages the project as whole and controls the deadlines and the allocated budget together with the other involved parties.
- Construction Manager: manages the construction process to see that the project gets completed on time and meetspecified quality standards.
- HR Manager: responsible for recruiting and training staff for new branches.
- Design Team (Interior Designer): Develops store plans and conceptual designs for the interior of the space to reinforce the brand identity and create a unique in-store experience for customers.
- Supply Chain Manager: Purchases and orders materials, equipment and supplies and coordinates their delivery.
- Finance Manager: Responsible for monitoring the finance of a project and maintaining financial control.
- Marketing Team: Plan & execute public promotion and advertising campaigns in order to create publicity and encourage sales during the time of a new store opening.
- Legal Advisor - Legal Compliance, Supplier/Contractor Contracts

Impacts (In Terms of Scope, Time, Cost/Budget, and Quality):

- Scope: Roles within the company contribute to the overall scope in relation to setting up operations, workforce planning and supply chain effectiveness.
- Time: The delays in the hiring process, the construction or procurement process could affect the project time scope negatively.
- Cost/Budget: The cost may be affected by supplier price variations, costs of labour and unforeseen problems.
- Quality: The success of a project heavily depend on the design quality standards, skill of the design and construction workforce, and also the operational efficiency of the supply chain.

Risk Response Plans:

- Mitigation: Pre-identify and prepare relevant suppliers and contractors to mitigate potential delays, and utilise local labour to reduce the challenges associated with manning.
- Contingen: Set aside 10% of the construction budget for unexpected expenses and continually monitor the building process to address problems promptly.
