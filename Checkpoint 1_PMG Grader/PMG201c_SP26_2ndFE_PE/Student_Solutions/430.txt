Request 1:
Project name: Coi Cafe brand

Cost/bubget: 8.5 billion VND

Open five Coi Cafe flagship stores in the Central region of Vietnam within 12 months, with one store located in each target province: Da Nang, Hue, Quang Nam, Quang Ngai, and Binh Dinh.

Specific: The objective is to complete store setup and launch one flagship store in each selected province.

Measurable: The result can be measured by the number of stores officially opened and operating.

Achievable: This target is reasonable because Coi Cafe already has experience managing 12 stores in Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City.

Relevant: The objective matches the company’s expansion plan and helps the brand reach new customers outside its current markets.

Time-bound: The full expansion should be completed within 12 months.


Recruit and train enough local employees for all five new stores before the official opening of each location.

Specific: The project needs to hire and train baristas, cashiers, service staff, and store supervisors for each new branch.

Measurable: This can be measured by the number of employees hired, trained, and approved for work.

Achievable: It is achievable if the HR team starts recruitment early and uses Coi’s existing training materials.

Relevant: Well-trained staff are necessary to maintain service quality and brand consistency.

Time-bound: Staff preparation should be completed before each store’s launch date.

Request 3:
1. Site Evaluation Review (QA):
Before choosing a store location, the project team should review customer traffic, rental cost, accessibility, nearby competitors, and local customer behavior. This helps the team avoid locations that look attractive at first but may not bring enough customers later.
2. Supplier Qualification Check (QA):
The team should evaluate potential suppliers before signing agreements. They need to check product quality, delivery reliability, price stability, and whether the supplier can support all five stores. This is important because unstable supply can directly affect store operations.
3. Design Standard Review (QA):
Before construction begins, the interior design should be reviewed by the project manager and design team. The review makes sure that each store still keeps Coi’s nostalgic atmosphere while also fitting the local space and customer expectations.
4. Construction and Installation Inspection (QC):
During construction, regular inspections should be done to check materials, furniture, lighting, safety, and equipment installation. If the team finds problems early, they can fix them before the store enters the final preparation stage.
5. Employee Training Assessment (QC):
After training, staff should be tested through practical tasks such as preparing drinks, serving customers, using the POS system, and handling customer complaints. This ensures that new employees can work properly before opening day.
6. Pre-launch Quality Check (QC):
A final checklist should be completed before the official opening. The team should check store cleanliness, menu availability, equipment function, staff readiness, and customer service flow. This reduces the chance of mistakes during the first launch days.
