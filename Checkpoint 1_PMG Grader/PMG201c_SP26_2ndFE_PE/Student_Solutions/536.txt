Request 1: Define at least 2 project objectives using SMART criteria
Objective 1:

The project aims to open five new Coi Cafe flagship stores in Central Vietnam, including Da Nang, Hue, Quang Nam, Quang Ngai, and Binh Dinh, within one year.

Specific: The project focuses on setting up one store in each selected province.
Measurable: Success can be measured by the number of stores opened and ready for daily operation.
Achievable: This goal is realistic because Coi Cafe already has 12 stores in Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City, so the company has basic experience in store expansion.
Relevant: This objective supports Coi’s plan to grow its brand outside the two biggest cities in Vietnam.
Time-bound: The five stores should be completed and launched within 12 months.

Objective 2:

The project also aims to prepare a stable operating system for all new stores before their official opening.

Specific: This includes staff recruitment, training, supplier coordination, store procedures, and launch preparation.
Measurable: The objective can be measured through completed training records, signed supplier agreements, and approved store operation checklists.
Achievable: It is achievable because the company can reuse part of its existing store model and adjust it for the Central region.
Relevant: A store cannot perform well if the team, materials, and operation process are not ready.
Time-bound: The operating system should be ready before each store opening date.

In-Scope Items:

Request 3: Define at least 5 quality assurance (QA) or quality control (QC) activities

1. Market and Location Review (QA):
Before signing rental contracts, the team should review each location carefully. They need to check customer traffic, rental price, nearby competitors, parking convenience, and whether the area fits the brand image. This helps reduce the chance of choosing a poor location.

2. Budget Review (QA):
The project team should review the budget plan for each store before major spending starts. Since the total budget is VND 8.5 billion, cost control is quite important. This review helps make sure that construction, rental, marketing, and staff costs are still within the approved budget.

3. Interior Design Approval (QA):
Each store design should be checked and approved before construction begins. The purpose is to make sure the design still reflects Cofi Cafe’s nostalgic and traditional style. If the design is not reviewed early, the store may look inconsistent with the brand.

4. Construction Quality Check (QC):
During the construction stage, the project team should inspect the site regularly. They should check finishing quality, layout, safety, lighting, furniture installation, and equipment placement. This helps detect mistakes early and avoids expensive rework near the opening date.

5. Staff Service Test (QC):
After training, employees should be tested through role-play or trial service. For example, they can practice taking orders, preparing drinks, handling complaints, and explaining menu items. This ensures staff are ready before dealing with real customers.

6. Soft Opening Review (QC):
Before the official launch, each store should have a soft opening for a small group of customers. The team can observe service speed, drink quality, customer feedback, and store workflow. Any serious issue should be fixed before the grand opening.
