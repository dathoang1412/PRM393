REQUEST 1

Project Name: Coi Cafe Central Region Expansion Project

Justifications:
-> Strategy: Launching 5 flagship stores in Da Nang, Hue, Quang Nam, Quang Ngai and Binh Dinh in the Central region of Vietnam.
-> Reasons: To capitalize on customer demand for sustainable business models, to address potential market saturation in the northern retail market in Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City, and to utilize our current direct supply chain from Dak Lak.

Project Constraints:
-> What's included: ALL YOU NEED TO KNOW - survey premises, design & build the interior, recruit and train local staff, establish the central supply chain, then run the full scale launch campaign.
-> Time: The total time for the project shall be 12 months, and break-even point for the individual store shall be reached within 8 months from opening.
-> Cost/Budget: Total investment for project will be VND 8.5 billion (USD 375,000) in total, and financed by a combination of both owner's equity and commercial bank loans, with an average investment of approximately VND 1.7 billion (USD 75,000) per store.
-> Quality: Exceed quality expectations by making the space and menu feel truly traditional, while also emphasizing the restaurant’s sustainability (reuse of cups, direct sourcing).

Recruitment & Training
-> Content: Job listings for current store manager and barista opportunities, as well as information on hiring fairs and training sessions for upcoming local store managers.

Inventory & Supply Chain Setup
-> We started off by getting the initial raw materials (coffee beans and cups), the machines needed to get started, and laid the groundwork for our future Central region operations.
-> Estimation: VND 2 billion.
-> Estimation Method: Three-point estimating, combined with direct supplier quotes. Managed by Market Development Director.

Risk 1: Construction and Setup Delays
-> Construction Delays: Due to weather, local permits or the lack thereof and various other reasons a renovation could be delayed.
-> This will cause the store opening date to be pushed back (Time), increase Labour Costs (Cost), and push the end of the 12 month project deadline.
-> Mitigation: Hire local reputable contractors to perform work. Include a delay penalty in contracts. Build in some buffer time into the plan.
-> Contingency: Fast-track critical trades to recover the baseline schedule or allow overtime to meet the schedule commitment.

Risk 2: Failur to ReachBreak-Even Within 8 Months
-> Footfall and Sales Revenue: Local customer traffic and sales revenues may not achieve initially projected levels.
-> Possible Impact: Strains project's cash flow (Cost) and delays loan repayment; in worst case projects a reduction in scope (Scope).
-> Mitigation: Conduct in-depth local market research prior to site selection; aggressive local market marketing efforts.
-> Fund: Shift unused contingency funding to support promotional discounts as well as adjust menu and promotional items to better suit local customer preferences.
