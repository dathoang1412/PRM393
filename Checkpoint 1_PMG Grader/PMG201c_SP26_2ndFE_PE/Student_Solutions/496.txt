Request 1: 
Project Name: Coi Cafe Central Region Expansion Project
Justifications (Purpose & Reasons): The purpose of this project is to capitalize on Coi Cafe's established brand success in Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City by expanding into the fast-growing Central Vietnam market. This expansion will increase market share, boost brand awareness nationwide, and leverage the company's sustainable business model to attract eco-conscious consumers in new regions.
Project Constraints:
Scope: Limited to opening exactly 5 flagship stores in 5 specific provinces (Da Nang, Hue, Quang Nam, Quang Ngai, Binh Dinh). Must utilize Central region suppliers for raw materials.
Time: All 5 stores must be open and operational within 12 months.
Cost/Budget: Strictly capped at VND 8.5 billion (averaging VND 1.7 billion per store). Funded via equity and commercial loans.
Quality: Must strictly adhere to the Coi Cafe brand identity (nostalgic space design, traditional drink menu, sustainable practices like reusable cups and direct-from-farmer coffee). Furthermore, each store must hit the financial quality metric of breaking even within 8 months of opening.
Request 2: 
Five main budget items for the project:
1. Premises Leasing & Deposits
Description: Costs associated with securing the physical locations for all 5 flagship stores, including initial deposits and the first few months of renwt.
Estimation: VND 1.5 billion.
How to Estimate: Analogous estimation. Using historical leasing data from current Hanoi/HCMC stores, adjusted for the lower real estate market rates in the Central provinces.
Person in Charge: Project Manager (Market Development Director).
2. Interior Design & Construction
Description: The cost of remodeling the leased spaces, building the "nostalgic" aesthetic, and purchasing furniture, fixtures, and brewing equipment.
Estimation: VND 4.5 billion.
How to Estimate: Parametric estimating. Multiplying the estimated cost per square meter of construction by the total square footage of the 5 stores.
Person in Charge: Interior Design Team Lead.
3. Local Supply Chain Setup & Initial Inventory
Description: Costs to scout, negotiate, and establish logistics with new Central region suppliers, plus purchasing the first batches of raw materials.
Estimation: VND 0.8 billion.
How to Estimate: Vendor bid analysis and expert judgment from current procurement specialists.
Person in Charge: Project Manager.
4. Recruitment & Training
Description: Costs for advertising job openings, interviewing, and running training programs to ensure local staff meet Coi Cafe's service and brand standards.
Estimation: VND 0.5 billion.
How to Estimate: Bottom-up estimation. Calculating the cost of job postings, trainer travel expenses, and trainee stipends per store, then aggregating them.
Person in Charge: HR Team Lead.
5. Marketing & Grand Opening Campaigns
Description: Digital marketing, local PR, promotional materials, and launch event costs to drive initial foot traffic to the nwew stores.
Estimation: VND 1.2 billion.
How to Estimate: Top-down estimation. Allocating a fixed percentage of the total project budget strictly for marketing purposes.
Person in Charge: Marketing Team Lead.
Request 3:
1. Risk: Supply Chain Establishment Delays
Description: The project team struggles to find local suppliers in the Central region who can meet Coi Cafe's strict quality and sustainability requirements within the required timeframe.
Impact:  Time: Delays the store opening dates.
Cost: Increases logistics costs if materials must be shipped from further away.
Mitigation: Begin vendor scouting and quality testing immediately at the start of the project; maintain a broad database of potential suppliers.
Contingency: Temporarily utilize the existing Dak Lak/HCMC supply chain to ship materials to the Central stores until local vendowr contracts are finalized.
2. Risk: Construction Budget Overruns
Description: The cwost of materials and labor for the interior construction exceeds the average allocationw of VND 1.7 billion per store due to local inflation or hidden premises damages.
Impact: Cost: Exhausts the VND 8.5 billion budget prematurely.
Quality/Scope: May require cutting corners on store design, violating brand consistency.
Mitigation: Cwonduct thorough site surveys before signing leases to avoid "fixer-upper" traps. Negotiate fixwed-price contracts with local construction vendors.
Contingency: Release contingency reserve funds; temporarily scale back non-essential decorative elements to stay under budget.
3. Risk: Failure to Meet the Break-Even Target
Description: The new stores fail to attract enough customers to reach the required break-even point within 8 months of opening, possibly due to strong local competition.
Impact: Quality/Scope: Fails a major financial constraint outlined in the project charter.
Mitigation: Conduct deep local market research in each of the 5 provinces; ensure the marketing team tailors the grand opening campaigns specifically to local demographics.
Contingency: Allocate emergency marketing funds for aggressive promotional discounts and loyalty programs in the underperforming stores.
