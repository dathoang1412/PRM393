Request 1:
Project name:
Coi Cafe Central Premium Flagship Experience Project

Justifications:
Expand Coi Cafe into five Central provinces: Da Nang, Hue, Quang Nam, Quang Ngai, and Binh Dinh.
Position each new store as a premium flagship location that strongly represents Coi Cafe’s Vietnamese coffee culture.
Attract customers who value atmosphere, brand story, product quality, and consistent service.
Build strong brand recognition in Central Vietnam before expanding further.
Generate new revenue and ensure each store reaches break-even within eight months after opening.

Project constraints:
Scope:
Open five premium flagship stores only, one store in each selected province.
Project scope includes: premium location assessment, lease agreement, flagship interior design, construction, equipment setup, staff recruitment, premium service training, supplier setup, launch campaign, and performance monitoring.
Time:
The project must be completed within 12 months.
All five premium flagship stores must be opened within the approved timeline.
Each store must reach break-even within eight months after opening.
Cost / Budget:
Total budget is VND 8.5 billion.
Average budget is approximately VND 1.7 billion per store.
Funding comes from company equity and commercial bank loans.
Spending must be controlled carefully because premium experience may increase construction and service preparation costs.
Quality:
All stores must meet premium flagship standards.
Interior design must be nostalgic, polished, and consistent with Coi Cafe’s brand identity.
Drink quality, service quality, hygiene, and sustainability practices must be consistent.
Staff must be trained to provide professional and warm customer service.

Name: Premium nostalgic interior and construction

Request 3:
Rist 1: Premium positioning is too expensive for local market
Risk name: Premium concept exceeds local willingness to pay.
Description: Some customers may appreciate the store design but may not be willing to pay higher prices for a premium cafe experience.
Impacts: 
Scope / quality: The premium flagship concept may not create enough sales.
Time: Break-even may take longer than eight months.
Cost: Additional promotions or price adjustments may reduce profit margin.
Mitigation plan: Conduct pricing research and competitor benchmarking before setting menu and promotion strategy.
Contingency plan: Introduce value combos, loyalty offers, and off-peak promotions while keeping core premium products unchanged.

Risk 2: Premium construction exceeds budget
Risk name: Premium interior costs are higher than planned.
Description: Higher-quality materials, decoration, lighting, and furniture may increase construction costs beyond the planned budget.
Impacts:
Scope / quality: Some premium design elements may need to be reduced.
Time: Re-design or material replacement may delay construction.
Cost: Total project budget may exceed VND 8.5 billion.
Mitigation plan: Approve a fixed design budget, compare contractor quotations, and separate must-have brand elements from optional premium details.
Contingency plan: Replace non-essential premium items with lower-cost alternatives while keeping the main nostalgic brand atmosphere.

Risk 3: Service quality does not match premium image
Risk name: Staff service fails to support premium positioning.
Description: If new employees are not well trained, customers may feel the service quality does not match the flagship store image.
Impacts:
Scope / quality: Customer satisfaction and brand reputation may decline.
Time: Additional coaching and service correction may be needed.
Cost: Extra training, supervision, and customer recovery cost may occur.
Mitigation plan: Conduct premium service training, role-play, drink quality tests, and pre-opening service evaluation.
Contingency plan: Assign experienced store supervisors from existing branches during the first month after opening.
