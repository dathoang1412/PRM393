REQUEST 1
Project name
Coi Cafe Central Vietnam Market Entry Project
The Coi Cafe Central Vietnam Market Entry Project is created to support the companys expansion into the Central region of Vietnam. The main goal of this project is to open five new flagship stores in five provinces and cities, including Da Nang, Hue, Quang Nam, Quang Ngai, and Binh Dinh. The project will be completed within 12 months. Each store will represent the image of Coi Cafe as a Vietnamese coffee brand with traditional drinks, nostalgic store design, reusable cups, and sustainable coffee sourcing from Dak Lak farmers
this project will include many important activities. The first activity is market research The project team will study customer needs, competitor coffee shops, local rental prices, and suitable business areas in each province. The second activity is store location selection. The team will find locations with good customer traffic, suitable rental cost, and enough space for a flagship store. The third activity is store design and construction. Each store must follow the same brand image but can still have small local details to make customers feel familiar
The project will also include equipment purchasing, supply chain preparation, staff recruitment, staff training, and marketing launch campaigns. The operations team will prepare machines, furniture, POS systems, raw materials, and delivery schedules. The HR team will recruit store managers, baristas, cashiers, and service staff. The marketing team will prepare opening events, online advertisements, and local promotion programs to attract customers during the first months
The Market Development Director will be the project manager. This person will be responsible for the whole project and will coordinate with all related teams. The project manager must control the timeline, budget, quality, communication, and risk management. The main project stakeholders include the companys management board, finance team, design and construction team, HR team, marketing team, operations team, suppliers, contractors, local employees, and customers
Project justification
This project is necessary because Coi Cafe needs to grow outside its current main markets. The company already has stores in Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City, so expanding to Central Vietnam is a logical next step. Central Vietnam has many potential customers, including students, office workers, tourists, families, and young people who enjoy coffee shops. Cities such as Da Nang and Hue are especially attractive because they have strong tourism, many universities, and active urban life
The project can help Coi Cafe increase brand awareness in new markets. If customers in Central Vietnam know and like the brand, the company can build a stronger national presence. Opening flagship stores is also useful because these stores can show the full identity of Coi Cafe. Customers can experience the store design, drink quality, service style, and sustainable values of the brand directly
This project also supports long term financial growth. If the five stores operate well, they can create new revenue for the company and reduce dependence on Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City. The company can also use the experience from this project to improve its future expansion plan. For example, the company can learn which location types work best, which marketing methods are effective, and how to train staff in new provinces
Another reason for the project is brand value. Coi Cafe focuses on Vietnamese coffee culture and sustainable sourcing from Dak Lak farmers. By opening more stores, the company can buy more coffee beans from farmers and promote Vietnamese coffee to more customers. The use of reusable cups and sustainable practices also helps the company build a modern and responsible image
Project constraints
The first constraint is scope. The project only includes five flagship stores in Da Nang, Hue, Quang Nam, Quang Ngai, and Binh Dinh. It does not include opening stores in other regions or opening more than five stores during this project period. The project also does not include changing the core brand identity of Coi Cafe. All new stores must follow the companys main style, product standards, and service standards
The second constraint is time. The project must be completed within 12 months. This is a challenging timeline because the team needs to complete many tasks such as market research, location selection, rental negotiation, design, construction, equipment purchase, recruitment, training, supply chain setup, and marketing launch. If one task is delayed, other tasks may also be affected. For example, if construction is delayed, staff training and grand opening may need to be moved later
The third constraint is budget. The total budget is VND 8.5 billion. This budget must cover five stores, so the project team must control spending carefully. The main cost items include rental deposit, design and construction, equipment, furniture, staff training, marketing, inventory, logistics, and contingency. Since the project may use both company capital and bank loans, overspending can create financial pressure for the company
The fourth constraint is quality. All stores must meet Coi Cafes standards. The drink taste must be consistent with current stores. The store design must show the nostalgic and Vietnamese identity of the brand. Staff must serve customers politely and professionally. The stores must also follow hygiene, safety, and sustainability requirements. If quality is not controlled well, the project may damage the brand instead of helping it grow.


REQUEST 2

Main cost and budget items

1. Market research and location survey cost

This cost includes collecting information about customers, competitors, traffic flow, rental prices, and possible store areas in each province. The team may need to travel to Da Nang, Hue, Quang Nam, Quang Ngai, and Binh Dinh to check real locations and understand the market. This cost can include transportation, accommodation, research tools, staff working time, and local consultant fees if needed.

This cost can be estimated by listing all research activities and calculating the cost for each province. For example, the team can estimate the number of survey days, number of staff members, travel costs, and research expenses. The Project Manager and Marketing team will be responsible for this estimate because they need to understand the market before selecting locations.

2. Rental deposit and leasing cost

This cost includes rental deposit, advance rental payment, legal review of lease contracts, and possible brokerage fees. Store location is very important for a coffee shop because it affects customer traffic and sales. A good location may cost more, but it can help the store attract more customers and reach break even faster.

This cost can be estimated by collecting rental quotations from landlords and property agents in each province. The project team should compare monthly rent, deposit months, contract duration, location quality, and payment conditions. The Project Manager and Finance team will be responsible for this cost item. They must make sure that rental cost is reasonable and suitable for the total budget.

3. Store design and construction cost

This cost includes design fee, construction work, flooring, ceiling, lighting, wall decoration, counter area, seating area, restroom, signboard, and other store finishing items. Because Coi Cafe has a nostalgic Vietnamese design style, this cost is very important. The stores should not only look beautiful but also create a comfortable atmosphere for customers.

This cost can be estimated by asking several contractors to provide quotations based on store size and design requirements. The Design and Construction team should compare quotations and check contractor experience before making a decision. The Project Manager should approve the final construction budget to make sure it does not exceed the planned amount.

4. Furniture and store equipment cost

This cost includes tables, chairs, shelves, coffee machines, grinders, refrigerators, blenders, water filters, POS systems, cameras, speakers, reusable cups, and other operating tools. These items are necessary for daily store operation. If the company buys low quality equipment, maintenance cost may increase later. If the company buys very expensive equipment, the project may exceed the budget.

This cost can be estimated by preparing a detailed list of equipment for one store and multiplying it by five stores. The Operations team and Procurement team should collect price quotations from suppliers and compare product quality, warranty, delivery time, and after sales service. The company should choose equipment that is reliable and suitable for long term use.

5. Recruitment and training cost

This cost includes job posting, interview activities, staff uniforms, training materials, trainer salary, and salary support during the training period. Each store needs a store manager, baristas, cashiers, and service staff. Training is very important because employees are the people who directly serve customers and represent the brand.

This cost can be estimated based on the number of employees needed for each store, the number of training days, uniform cost, training material cost, and trainer cost. The HR team will be responsible for preparing this estimate. The Operations team can support the HR team by explaining the number of staff needed for each position.

6. Marketing and grand opening cost

This cost includes online advertising, local posters, banners, grand opening events, discount vouchers, free trial drinks, influencer cooperation, and customer loyalty programs. Since many customers in Central Vietnam may not know Coi Cafe yet, marketing is necessary to create awareness and attract first time customers.

This cost can be estimated by creating a marketing plan for each store. The Marketing team should decide the advertising channels, event scale, promotion budget, and expected number of customers. The estimate should be different for each province because customer behavior and market size are not the same. For example, Da Nang may need a larger launch campaign because it is a larger and more competitive market.

7. Initial inventory and logistics cost

This cost includes coffee beans, milk, tea, sugar, cups, packaging, cleaning materials, and transportation. It also includes preparing delivery schedules from suppliers to the five new stores. Since Coi Cafe uses coffee from Dak Lak farmers, the company must make sure that the supply chain is stable before opening.

This cost can be estimated based on expected first month sales, average material usage per drink, supplier prices, storage needs, and transportation costs. The Operations team will be responsible for this item. They must also prepare backup suppliers and safety stock to reduce the risk of shortage.
REQUEST 3

Project risks and response plans

Risk 1

Weak customer awareness in new markets

Because Coi Cafe is mainly known in Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City, customers in Central Vietnam may not know the brand well. Some customers may already have favorite local coffee shops, while others may choose cheaper coffee brands. This can make sales lower than expected during the first months after opening. If sales are low, the stores may not reach break even within the expected time.

This risk can affect cost because the company may need to spend more money on advertising, promotions, and discounts. It can also affect time because the payback period may become longer. In terms of quality, the company should not reduce product or service quality just to save money because this can hurt the brand image.

The mitigation plan is to conduct detailed market research before opening. The Marketing team should study local customer behavior, competitor prices, popular drinks, and suitable promotion channels. The company should create local marketing campaigns for each province instead of using only one general campaign. The contingency plan is to increase local promotions, work with local influencers, improve delivery service, and add some suitable local menu options while keeping the main Coi Cafe identity.

Risk 2

Construction delay or poor construction quality

Construction delay can happen because of contractor problems, late materials, bad weather, design changes, or local approval issues. If construction is delayed, the opening date will also be delayed. This may increase rental cost because the company may already pay rent before the store starts selling. Marketing campaigns and staff training may also need to be rescheduled.

Poor construction quality is another serious risk. If the store is built too quickly or the materials are not good enough, the store may not match the image of Coi Cafe. Customers may feel that the store is not comfortable or not professional. Repairing construction mistakes after opening can cost more money and may disturb daily business.

The mitigation plan is to choose reliable contractors with good experience in building coffee shops or retail stores. The contract should clearly state deadlines, payment terms, quality standards, and penalties for delay. The Design and Construction team should check progress every week. The contingency plan is to prepare backup contractors, buy important materials early, and use contingency budget for urgent problems.

Risk 3

Staff performance does not meet brand standards

New employees in Central Vietnam may not understand Coi Cafes service style, drink recipes, and brand values at the beginning. If training is not strong enough, drinks may taste different from current stores, service may be slow, and customers may not be satisfied. This risk directly affects customer experience and brand reputation.

This risk can increase cost because the company may need to spend more money on retraining, replacing employees, or sending experienced staff from other cities. It can also affect time because the store may need a longer soft opening period before official opening. If staff quality is poor, customers may leave negative reviews, which can affect future sales.

The mitigation plan is to prepare a detailed training program before opening. Training should include drink preparation, product knowledge, customer service, cashier process, hygiene rules, complaint handling, and brand story. Experienced employees from Hanoi or Ho Chi Minh City can support training in the new stores. The contingency plan is to send trainers again after opening, use mystery customer checks, and review staff performance weekly during the first two months.
