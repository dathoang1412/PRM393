Request 1:

Project name:
Coi Cafe Central Service Excellence Expansion Project

Justifications:
Expand Coi Cafe into five Central provinces: Da Nang, Hue, Quang Nam, Quang Ngai, and Binh Dinh.
Focus on service excellence as the main competitive advantage of the five new flagship stores.
Ensure that new local staff can deliver the same customer service quality, drink preparation standards, and sustainable practices as existing Coi Cafe stores.
Introduce Coi Cafe’s nostalgic Vietnamese coffee space, traditional drink menu, reusable cup model, and direct coffee sourcing from Dak Lak farmers to Central Vietnam.
Build customer trust in new markets through consistent service, friendly staff, stable drink quality, and professional store operations.
Develop a training-based expansion model that can be reused for future store openings.
Increase revenue outside Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City and help each store reach break-even within eight months after opening.

Project constraints:
Scope:
Open five flagship stores only, one store in each selected province.
Project scope includes: market survey, premises selection, lease agreement, interior design, construction, equipment setup, recruitment, service training, supplier setup, launch campaign, and post-opening service monitoring.
The project does not include franchising, extra stores, new product lines, or expansion to provinces outside the assigned five.
Time:
The project duration is 12 months.
All five stores must be opened within the approved timeline.
Recruitment and training must be completed before each store’s opening.
Each store must reach break-even within eight months after opening.
Cost / Budget:
Total budget is VND 8.5 billion.
Average budget is approximately VND 1.7 billion per store.
Funding comes from company equity and commercial bank loans.
Cost must be controlled carefully, especially for construction, equipment, training, inventory, and launch promotion.
Quality:
All stores must follow Coi Cafe’s brand standards.
Interior design must reflect the nostalgic Vietnamese coffee concept.
Drink quality, hygiene, reusable cup practice, and customer service must be consistent across all five stores.
Each store must pass a service readiness check before opening.

Request 3:

Risk 1: New staff cannot meet service standards
Risk name: Weak service readiness before opening.
Description: Local employees may not fully understand Coi Cafe’s service style, drink preparation process, reusable cup practice, or customer care standards.
Impacts:
Scope / quality: Service quality may be inconsistent and damage the flagship store image.
Time: Opening may be delayed if staff cannot pass training.
Cost: Extra training, trainer travel, and temporary staff support may increase costs.
Mitigation plan: Use a standard training program, practical drink tests, service role-play, and pre-opening evaluation for all staff.
Contingency plan: Send experienced staff from Hanoi or Ho Chi Minh City to support the new stores during the first month.

Risk 2: Store layout causes slow service
Risk name: Inefficient service flow.
Description: If counter layout, seating arrangement, equipment placement, or order flow is not designed well, service speed may be slow during peak hours.
Impacts:
Scope / quality: Customer satisfaction and service quality may decrease.
Time: Additional layout adjustment may be required before or after opening.
Cost: Rework, extra equipment movement, or additional staff cost may occur.
Mitigation plan: Design store layout based on service flow and test the ordering process before launch.
Contingency plan: Adjust counter setup, add temporary staff during peak hours, and revise customer waiting flow.

Risk 3: Customer satisfaction is low after launch
Risk name: Poor early customer experience.
Description: Customers may complain about waiting time, drink taste, staff attitude, price, or store comfort during the first months.
Impacts:
Scope / quality: The store may fail to build strong loyalty in the local market.
Time: More time may be needed to stabilize customer traffic.
Cost: Additional service recovery, training, and promotion cost may be required.
Mitigation plan: Collect customer feedback daily during the first month and review service quality weekly.
Contingency plan: Provide service recovery vouchers, retrain staff, adjust operating process, and increase store manager supervision.
