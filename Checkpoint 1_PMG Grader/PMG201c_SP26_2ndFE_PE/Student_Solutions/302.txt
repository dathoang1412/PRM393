Request 1: Project Charter
1. Project Name

Coi Café – Central Vietnam Expansion Strategy (2026)

2. Project Justification
Expand Coi Café’s footprint into Central Vietnam to achieve broader market coverage.
Meet increasing demand for authentic Vietnamese coffee combined with environmentally conscious business practices.
Strengthen brand equity through the development of flagship stores in strategically selected provinces.
Diversify sourcing channels by incorporating suppliers from Central Vietnam.
Enhance long-term profitability and reduce reliance on existing core markets.
3. Project Constraints

Scope

Establish five flagship stores in Da Nang, Hue, Quang Nam, Quang Ngai, and Binh Dinh.
Scope includes site selection, design and construction, recruitment and training, supply chain development, and marketing rollout.

Time

Total project duration: 12 months maximum.
Each store must reach break-even within 8 months after opening.

Budget

Total investment: VND 8.5 billion (~VND 1.7 billion per store).
Funding sources include equity capital and bank loans.

Quality

Maintain consistent brand identity with nostalgic café ambiance and traditional menu offerings.
Ensure sustainable operations through eco-friendly materials and ethical sourcing.
Guarantee service consistency through structured employee training.
Request 2: Cost Structure Overview
1. Leasing and Store Setup
Description: Rental deposits, lease payments, interior construction, and decoration.
Estimated Cost: ~VND 700 million per store
Estimation Method: Based on historical benchmarking and contractor quotations
Responsible: Project Manager & Design Team
2. Equipment and Furnishings
Description: Coffee machines, brewing equipment, refrigeration units, and furniture.
Estimated Cost: ~VND 300 million per store
Estimation Method: Bottom-up costing using supplier price data
Responsible: Procurement & Operations
3. Human Resource Costs
Description: Recruitment, salaries, onboarding, and training programs.
Estimated Cost: ~VND 200 million per store
Estimation Method: Parametric estimation based on staffing structure and wage levels
Responsible: HR Department
4. Marketing and Launch Activities
Description: Advertising campaigns, promotional activities, and opening events.
Estimated Cost: ~VND 150 million per store
Estimation Method: Based on previous campaign performance and revenue projections
Responsible: Marketing Department
5. Supply Chain Setup
Description: Supplier onboarding, logistics coordination, and initial inventory procurement.
Estimated Cost: ~VND 150 million per store
Estimation Method: Supplier quotations and pilot sourcing agreements
Responsible: Supply Chain Management
Request 3: Risk Analysis and Response Plan
Risk 1: Construction Delays
Description: Delays caused by permitting issues, contractor inefficiency, or material shortages.
Impacts:
Time: Schedule extension
Cost: Increased expenses
Quality: Risk of reduced construction quality
Response:
Mitigation: Strict contractor selection and progress tracking
Contingency: Buffer time of 2–3 weeks per store
Risk 2: Revenue Underperformance
Description: Stores may not reach expected revenue targets within the planned timeframe.
Impacts:
Cost: Lower profitability
Time: Delayed break-even point
Response:
Mitigation: Market research and strong pre-opening marketing
Contingency: Pricing adjustments and increased promotions
Risk 3: Supply Chain Disruption
Description: Unstable supply quality or delivery delays from suppliers.
Impacts:
Quality: Inconsistent products
Cost: Higher procurement costs
Time: Operational interruptions
Response:
Mitigation: Multiple suppliers and quality control system
Contingency: Backup suppliers in Dak Lak
