REQUEST 1:
Project name: Coi Cafe Five-Province Rollout Project.
This project focuses on launching 5 flagship Coi Cafe stores across Central Vietnam, specifically in Da Nang, Hue, Quang Nam, Quang Ngai, and Binh Dinh within a 12-month period. The main objective is to extend the company’s footprint beyond its current cities and capture new customer segments. The Central region shows strong tourism activity and a rising urban population, which creates demand for café experiences. In addition, the project helps strengthen the company’s sustainable model by sourcing materials closer to the new stores, improving efficiency and supporting local producers.
The project is limited by several key constraints. Regarding scope: Only five stores will be delivered, and each must strictly follow the established brand identity, including store layout, menu, and service approach. Regarding time: The full rollout must be completed within 12 months, so delays in one phase can affect the entire plan. Regarding cost: The budget is capped at VND 8.5 billion, which requires careful allocation for each store. Regarding quality: Consistency is critical, as all new locations must match the existing standards in product quality, customer experience, and design.
REQUEST 3:
Risk 1: Demand overestimation
Description: Expected customer volume is higher than actual demand.
Impact: Revenue lower than plan (cost pressure), longer break-even time, possible reduction in service quality due to cost cutting.
Response:
Mitigation: Conduct realistic demand forecasting and competitor analysis.
Contingency: Introduce promotions, adjust pricing strategy, optimize operating hours.
Risk 2: Coordination issues between teams
Description: Miscommunication between design, HR, and marketing teams.
Impact: Delays in timeline, inconsistent quality output, increased cost due to rework.
Response:
Mitigation: Set clear communication plan, regular meetings, shared progress tracking.
Contingency: PM intervenes directly, reassign responsibilities if needed.
Risk 3: Regulatory or legal delays
Description: Delays in permits or local approvals.
Impact: Schedule delay, additional administrative cost, possible change in project scope.
Response:
Mitigation: Prepare documents early, understand local regulations in each province.
Contingency: Adjust project sequence or prioritize other locations first.
