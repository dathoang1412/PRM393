Request 1:

Project name:
Coi Cafe Central Regional Operating Cluster Project

Justifications:
Expand Coi Cafe into five Central provinces: Da Nang, Hue, Quang Nam, Quang Ngai, and Binh Dinh.
Build the five flagship stores as one connected Central operating cluster instead of five separate store projects.
Improve management efficiency through shared standards for supply chain, staff training, equipment, reporting, and launch execution.
Strengthen Coi Cafe’s Vietnamese coffee identity through nostalgic store design, traditional menu, reusable cups, and direct sourcing from Dak Lak farmers.
Create a stable regional base for future expansion in nearby provinces.
Increase brand coverage and revenue outside Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City.
Support the goal that each store reaches break-even within eight months after opening.

Project constraints:
Scope:
Open five flagship stores only, one store in each assigned province.
Project scope includes: cluster planning, premises survey, location selection, lease agreement, standard interior design, construction, equipment setup, staff recruitment, training, supplier setup, launch campaign, and performance reporting.
The project does not include franchising, extra stores, new product lines, or expansion outside the five selected provinces.
Time:
The total project duration is 12 months.
All five stores must be opened within the approved timeline.
Shared operating standards and supply arrangements must be ready before store opening.
Each store must reach break-even within eight months after opening.
Cost / Budget:
Total budget is VND 8.5 billion.
Average budget is approximately VND 1.7 billion per store.
Funding comes from equity and commercial bank loans.
Budget must be controlled across the whole cluster, not only by individual store.
Quality:
All stores must follow the same Coi Cafe operating and brand standards.
Interior design, drink recipes, service quality, hygiene, and sustainability practices must be consistent across the cluster.
Store data and customer feedback must be monitored after launch to improve performance.

Request 2:

Expense Line 1: Cluster planning and market review
Name: Regional cluster planning and market review
Description: Cost for evaluating customer demand, province priority, competitor presence, location potential, and operating requirements for a connected five-store cluster.
Estimation: VND 650 million total, around VND 130 million per store.
Way to estimate: Based on field survey cost, travel expenses, market data collection, competitor checking, and cluster planning workshops.
Person in charge: Project Manager and Marketing Team.

Name: Premises search and lease package
Description: Cost for site search, traffic analysis, legal checking, lease negotiation, rental deposit, and first rent payment for five stores.
Estimation: VND 1.5 billion total, around VND 300 million per store.
Way to estimate: Based on rental rates, deposit terms, broker fees, legal review fees, and initial rent in each province.

Name: Standard construction and interior fit-out
Description: Cost for construction, furniture, lighting, counter, seating area, signage, nostalgic decoration, and standardized flagship finishing.

Way to estimate: Based on standard design package, store area, contractor quotations, material prices, and furniture list.

Name: Equipment, staff training, and operations setup

Way to estimate: Based on equipment checklist, supplier quotations, number of staff, training schedule, trainer cost, uniform cost, and salary support.
Person in charge: HR Team, Procurement Team, and Operations Team.

Expense Line 5: Regional supply and launch execution
Name: Regional supply chain and launch execution

Person in charge: Procurement and Supply Chain Team, and Marketing Team.

Request 3:

Risk 1: Cluster coordination becomes complicated
Risk name: Weak coordination across five provinces.
Description: Managing five stores as one cluster may create communication problems between construction, HR, procurement, and marketing teams.
Impacts:
Scope / quality: Some stores may miss required standards or preparation steps.
Time: Delays may happen if teams wait for shared decisions.
Cost: Rework, urgent delivery, or extra supervision cost may increase.
Mitigation plan: Use one master schedule, weekly coordination meetings, and clear approval points for each store.
Contingency plan: Assign temporary province coordinators to solve local issues faster and report directly to the Project Manager.

Risk 2: Shared supply plan fails
Risk name: Regional supply chain cannot support all stores.
Description: A shared supplier or delivery route may not be able to support five stores consistently after opening.
Impacts:
Scope / quality: Menu availability and drink quality may be affected.
Time: Store operations may be interrupted.
Cost: Emergency sourcing and express delivery may increase cost.
Mitigation plan: Use backup suppliers, test delivery capacity before opening, and prepare minimum safety stock for each store.
Contingency plan: Split delivery routes by province and temporarily use suppliers from existing stores or Dak Lak.

Risk 3: Performance gap between stores
Risk name: Some stores perform much weaker than others.
Description: Even with the same operating model, store performance may differ because of location, customer behavior, or competitor strength.
Impacts:
Scope / quality: The cluster may not achieve balanced brand presence.
Time: Weak stores may take longer to reach break-even.
Cost: Additional marketing or operational support may be needed.
Mitigation plan: Track sales, customer traffic, and feedback weekly for each store.
Contingency plan: Provide targeted support to weak stores, including local campaigns, menu bundles, and operating cost review.
